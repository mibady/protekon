title: "FAQ: What triggers a Cal/OSHA inspection?"
faqCategory: cal-osha
excerpt: "Cal/OSHA inspections are triggered by employee complaints, serious injury reports, referrals from other agencies, programmed inspections, and follow-ups on prior violations."
---

Cal/OSHA does not show up randomly. There is always a trigger. Understanding what puts you on their radar is the first step to staying off it.

**The five primary inspection triggers, in priority order:**

1. **Employee complaints.** This is the number one trigger. Any employee -- current or former -- can file a complaint alleging unsafe conditions. Cal/OSHA is required to respond. Complaints can be filed anonymously. You will not know who filed it. You will only know an inspector is at your door.

2. **Serious injury and illness reports.** California law requires employers to report any serious injury, illness, or fatality to Cal/OSHA within eight hours. Failure to report is itself a violation. Every report triggers an investigation.

3. **Referrals from other agencies.** Fire departments, police, building inspectors, workers' compensation carriers, other regulatory agencies -- any of them can refer a concern to Cal/OSHA. If a fire marshal notices safety issues during a building inspection, Cal/OSHA may hear about it.

4. **Programmed inspections.** Cal/OSHA conducts targeted inspections of high-hazard industries based on injury data, industry classification, and emphasis programs. If your NAICS code puts you in a high-hazard category, you can be selected for inspection without any complaint or incident.

5. **Follow-up inspections.** If Cal/OSHA previously cited you, expect a follow-up to verify you corrected the violations. Failing to abate cited hazards leads to additional penalties that compound daily.

**The practical reality:**

Most inspections are complaint-driven. This means your biggest exposure is an unhappy employee who knows you are cutting corners on safety. The best defense against a complaint-triggered inspection is not having anything to complain about.

Treat every day as if an inspector could walk in tomorrow. Because they can.
