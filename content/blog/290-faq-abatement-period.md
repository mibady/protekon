# FAQ: What is an abatement period and what happens if I miss it?

When Cal/OSHA issues a citation, they do not just fine you. They give you a deadline to fix the hazard. That deadline is the abatement period. Miss it, and you enter a penalty structure that makes the original fine look like a rounding error.

**What the abatement period is:**
A specific deadline — set by the inspector — by which you must correct the cited hazard. It could be immediate (for imminent dangers) or weeks/months (for hazards requiring engineering controls or equipment procurement).

**What happens if you miss it:**
Failure to abate is a separate violation with penalties of up to $15,000 per day for each day the hazard continues past the abatement deadline. That is not a typo. Per day.

A hazard that goes unabated for 30 days past deadline generates up to $450,000 in additional penalties — on top of the original citation amount. This is how a $25,000 serious violation becomes a six-figure catastrophe.

**How to protect yourself:**

First, take the abatement deadline seriously from day one. Build a corrective action plan immediately upon receiving the citation. Assign an owner, set internal milestones, and track progress.

Second, if you genuinely cannot meet the deadline — the equipment is backordered, the engineering firm needs more time, the construction permits are delayed — request an extension. File a Petition for Modification of Abatement (PMA) before the deadline. You must demonstrate good faith effort and interim protective measures.

Cal/OSHA is far more reasonable with employers who communicate proactively than with employers who go silent and miss deadlines.

**The bottom line:** The citation penalty is a one-time cost. Failure to abate is a daily cost. Fix the hazard or formally request more time. Ignoring the deadline is the most expensive option available.
