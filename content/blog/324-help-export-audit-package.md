# Exporting Your Compliance Audit Package

The inspector is here. The insurance auditor is on the phone. Your general contractor wants proof of your safety program before you start on their site. You need your compliance documentation organized, complete, and professional — and you need it in the next five minutes.

Protekon's Audit Package Export gives you exactly that.

## When You Need an Audit Package

Common scenarios for audit package exports:

- **Cal/OSHA inspection** — Inspector requests your IIPP, WVPP, training records, OSHA 300 Log, and incident reports
- **Insurance audit** — Carrier reviews your safety program for underwriting or renewal
- **General contractor prequalification** — GC requires proof of safety program before awarding a subcontract
- **Client due diligence** — Client requires evidence of your compliance programs before engaging your services
- **Internal audit** — Annual compliance review or board reporting
- **Legal request** — Attorney requests compliance documentation for litigation support

## Building Your Export

Navigate to **Documents > Export Audit Package.**

### Step 1: Select Date Range

Choose the time period for time-based documents (training records, incident reports, inspection records):

- **Last 12 months** (most common for inspections and insurance audits)
- **Last 24 months**
- **Last 36 months**
- **Calendar year** (select specific year)
- **Custom range** (specify start and end dates)

Your compliance plans (WVPP, IIPP, vertical programs) are always included in their current active version regardless of the date range selected.

### Step 2: Select Included Documents

Check the boxes next to the document categories you want to include:

**Core Plans (always recommended):**
- Active WVPP
- Active IIPP
- Active Vertical-Specific Programs
- Emergency Action Plan

**Regulatory Records:**
- OSHA 300 Log (for selected date range)
- OSHA 300A Annual Summary
- OSHA 301 Incident Reports (redacted by default)

**Training Documentation:**
- Training completion summary (aggregate)
- Individual training records (selectable by employee or department)
- Training certificates
- Training materials used

**Incident Records:**
- Incident reports (redacted by default — see PII options below)
- Investigation reports
- Corrective action summaries

**Inspection Records:**
- Workplace inspection checklists
- Hazard correction tracking
- Equipment inspection logs

**Assessment Records:**
- Phase 1 and Phase 2 compliance assessment results
- Gap analysis reports

### Step 3: PII Redaction Options

For each document category that contains personal information, select your redaction level:

- **Full Redaction** — All PII removed. Suitable for general distribution, insurance carriers, and contractor prequalification.
- **Partial Redaction** — Names and personal details removed, but job titles and incident specifics retained. Suitable for internal audits.
- **No Redaction** — Full detail included. Suitable for legal counsel (protected by privilege) and regulatory inspectors during formal inspections.

Protekon defaults to Full Redaction for external exports. You must explicitly select a lower redaction level, and that selection is logged in the audit trail.

### Step 4: Export Format

Choose your output format:

**PDF Package (single file):**
- All documents combined into a single PDF
- Includes cover page with company information and compliance score
- Table of contents with hyperlinked sections
- Section dividers between document categories
- Page numbers throughout
- Footer with "Generated by Protekon" and the date on every page

**ZIP Archive (multiple files):**
- Each document exported as an individual PDF
- Organized into folders matching the category structure
- Includes an index file listing all documents with descriptions
- Better for recipients who need to file documents separately or forward individual items

**Print-Ready:**
- PDF formatted for double-sided printing
- Includes print instructions and suggested binding
- Tab divider pages between sections (for physical binder assembly)

### Step 5: Generate and Download

Click **"Generate Audit Package."**

For large packages (100 or more pages), generation may take 30 to 60 seconds. Protekon displays a progress bar and notifies you when the package is ready for download.

The generated package is saved in your Document Library under **Documents > Audit Packages** with the generation date and a summary of included documents.

## Sharing Your Export

After generating, you have three sharing options:

**Direct download:** Click "Download" to save the file to your device.

**Email:** Click "Share via Email." Enter the recipient's email address. Add a message (optional). The recipient receives a secure download link (expires in 7 days by default — configurable).

**Secure link:** Click "Generate Secure Link." Copy the link and share it via any channel. Set link expiration (1 day, 7 days, 30 days, or custom). Optionally require the recipient to enter their email before downloading (creates an access log entry).

## Tips for Inspection Readiness

- **Pre-build your inspection package.** Do not wait for the inspector to arrive. Build a standard inspection package quarterly and keep it current.
- **Include a cover letter.** Add a brief overview of your safety program, your compliance score, and your designated contacts. First impressions matter.
- **Know what inspectors want.** Cal/OSHA inspectors typically request: IIPP, WVPP (if applicable), OSHA 300 Log, training records for the last year, and any incident reports related to the inspection purpose.
- **Keep a tablet ready.** Load your Protekon dashboard on a tablet and have it at reception. When the inspector arrives, hand it to your compliance manager. Digital access is faster than binder-flipping.

Being prepared is not the same as being compliant. But being unprepared guarantees you look non-compliant, even when you are.
