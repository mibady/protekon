# FAQ: Must safety training be provided in languages other than English?

Yes. This is not a suggestion. California law requires that safety training be delivered in a language the employee understands. Full stop.

Here is what that means in practice:

**The legal standard:** Cal/OSHA regulations state training must be provided "in a language readily understood by the affected employee." If your workforce includes Spanish speakers, Mandarin speakers, Tagalog speakers, or any other language group — they must receive training they can actually comprehend.

**How inspectors verify this:** Cal/OSHA inspectors routinely interview employees in their primary language. They ask what training was received, what topics were covered, and what procedures to follow in an emergency. If your Spanish-speaking employee cannot answer basic safety questions because training was delivered only in English, you have a citable violation.

**What counts as compliant:**
- Training delivered by a bilingual instructor in the employee's language
- Translated training materials with qualified interpretation
- Language-specific online training modules
- Bilingual toolbox talks with materials in both languages

**What does NOT count:**
- English-only training with a "buddy system" where bilingual employees explain it later
- Handing someone translated materials without actual instruction
- Assuming employees understand English because they nod along

**The business reality:** In California, particularly in construction, agriculture, manufacturing, and food service, multilingual workforces are the norm. English-only training programs are not just legally deficient — they are dangerously ineffective.

An employee who does not understand the lockout/tagout procedure because it was taught in a language they do not speak is an employee who can get killed.

Invest in proper multilingual training. The cost of translation is nothing compared to the cost of a serious injury, a citation, or a wrongful death lawsuit.

This is one area where doing the right thing and doing the smart thing are exactly the same thing.
