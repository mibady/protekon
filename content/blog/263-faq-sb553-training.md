title: "FAQ: What training does SB 553 require?"
faqCategory: sb-553
excerpt: "SB 553 requires initial training for all employees, annual refresher training, and additional training when plans change or new hazards emerge. Training must be bilingual and documented."
---

SB 553 training is not a one-time checkbox. It is an ongoing obligation with specific triggers that most employers underestimate.

**Required training events:**

1. **Initial training.** Every employee must receive training on your Workplace Violence Prevention Plan when they are first hired or when the plan is first established. This is non-negotiable.

2. **Annual refresher training.** At minimum, every twelve months. Not "when we get around to it." Not "during our annual safety meeting if we remember." Every year, documented, for every covered employee.

3. **Additional training when triggered.** You must provide supplemental training whenever:
   - A new workplace violence hazard is identified
   - The WVPP is updated or revised
   - A workplace violence incident occurs
   - New procedures or protocols are introduced

**What the training must cover:**

- The contents of your specific WVPP
- How to report workplace violence concerns, threats, and incidents
- How to recognize workplace violence hazards and warning signs
- Procedures for responding to workplace violence emergencies
- Employee rights under the law, including the right to report without retaliation

**The bilingual requirement:**

Training must be provided in a language and at a literacy level that employees can understand. If you have a workforce that includes non-English speakers, your training materials and delivery must accommodate them. Handing a Spanish-speaking warehouse crew an English-only PowerPoint does not satisfy this requirement.

**Documentation is everything:**

If you cannot prove the training happened, it did not happen. Maintain sign-in sheets with dates, topics covered, trainer identification, and employee signatures. Keep these records for the duration of employment plus three years.

Cal/OSHA inspectors will ask for training records. Have them ready.
