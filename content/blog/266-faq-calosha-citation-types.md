title: "FAQ: What are the different types of Cal/OSHA citations?"
faqCategory: cal-osha
excerpt: "Cal/OSHA issues five citation types: General ($18K), Serious ($25K), Willful ($180K), Repeat (enhanced), and Regulatory for posting and recordkeeping violations."
---

Cal/OSHA citations are not all created equal. The type of citation determines the penalty, and the differences are substantial.

**General violation -- up to $18,000.**

A condition that violates a Cal/OSHA standard but is not likely to cause death or serious physical harm. Missing safety data sheets, incomplete written programs, minor posting violations. Do not let the word "general" fool you -- $18,000 per violation adds up quickly when an inspector finds multiple issues.

**Serious violation -- up to $25,000.**

A hazard exists that could cause death or serious physical harm, and the employer knew or should have known about it. This is the most common citation type during inspections triggered by complaints or incidents. "Should have known" is a low bar. If a reasonable employer in your industry would have recognized the hazard, you should have too.

**Willful violation -- up to $180,000.**

The employer intentionally violated a standard or was plainly indifferent to employee safety. This is the nuclear option. Willful citations often involve situations where the employer was previously warned or cited for the same hazard and did nothing. If a willful violation results in a fatality, criminal prosecution is on the table.

**Repeat violation -- enhanced penalties.**

A substantially similar violation found within five years of a previous citation. Repeat classifications multiply the base penalty. Cal/OSHA tracks your history. They will know.

**Regulatory violation.**

Posting and recordkeeping violations -- failure to display required notices, maintain OSHA 300 logs, or post the annual summary. Lower penalties, but they signal to inspectors that your safety program is weak, which invites deeper scrutiny.

Every citation type creates a documented history that follows your business. The first citation is expensive. The second is worse.
