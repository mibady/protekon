title: "FAQ: What is the deadline for SB 553 compliance?"
faqCategory: sb-553
excerpt: "The SB 553 compliance deadline was July 1, 2024. If you missed it, you are operating out of compliance right now with no grace period."
---

The deadline was July 1, 2024. It has passed.

If you do not have a Workplace Violence Prevention Plan in place right now, you are not "behind schedule." You are operating in violation of California law. There is no grace period. There is no extension. There is no "we are working on it" defense that Cal/OSHA recognizes.

**What should have been in place by July 1, 2024:**

- A written Workplace Violence Prevention Plan specific to your workplace(s)
- A completed workplace violence hazard assessment
- A violent incident log
- Employee training on the plan, hazard recognition, and reporting procedures
- Documentation of all of the above

**What happens if you missed the deadline:**

You are exposed. Right now. Every day without a compliant plan is a day you could receive a Cal/OSHA citation. Every employee complaint that reaches Cal/OSHA will trigger an inspection. And the first thing an inspector will ask for is your written WVPP.

"We did not know" is not a defense. "We are a small company" is not a defense. "We have never had an incident" is not a defense.

The only thing that matters is whether you can produce a compliant plan, evidence of training, and a maintained incident log when asked.

**If you are reading this and you have not started:** Start today. Not next quarter. Not after your busy season. Today. The longer you wait, the more exposure you accumulate, and the harder it becomes to demonstrate good faith if an inspector does show up.

The deadline is not coming. It came. Act accordingly.
