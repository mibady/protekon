# FAQ: Can Protekon manage compliance for multiple locations?

Yes. The Multi-Site tier exists specifically for this. And if you are managing compliance across multiple locations with binders, spreadsheets, and hope — you already know why you need it.

**The multi-location problem:**

Each location needs its own customized IIPP. Your warehouse in Fontana has different hazards than your office in downtown Riverside. A cookie-cutter plan that treats both sites identically is not compliant — it is a liability. Cal/OSHA inspectors know the difference between a site-specific plan and a copy-paste job.

Each location also needs its own WVPP, because the workplace violence risk factors differ by site — a public-facing retail location has different threats than a secured distribution center.

**What Multi-Site includes:**

- **Site-specific compliance plans** — every location gets an IIPP and WVPP customized to its actual hazards, layout, and operations
- **Consolidated reporting** — one dashboard that shows compliance status across all locations, so you can see which sites are current and which are falling behind
- **Centralized training management** — schedule and track training across all sites from one system, ensuring no location misses a deadline
- **Consistent documentation standards** — every site documents incidents, training, and inspections the same way, so your records hold up under scrutiny regardless of which location gets inspected

**Why this matters:**

Cal/OSHA does not inspect your company. They inspect a location. If Site #3 has outdated training records because nobody was paying attention, Site #1's perfect compliance does not help you. Each location stands on its own during an inspection.

Multi-site operations need a system, not individual efforts. One location failing an inspection reflects on the entire organization — and triggers inspections at your other sites.

One system. Every location. Nothing falls through the cracks.
