# Manufacturing LOTO Enforcement Wave

Lockout/Tagout has been in OSHA's top ten most-cited standards for twenty years straight. You would think employers would get the message. They have not, and OSHA is escalating.

## What Changed

OSHA has launched a targeted enforcement initiative focused on Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) compliance in manufacturing facilities. The initiative responds to a cluster of amputation and fatality incidents in 2025 linked to inadequate energy control procedures.

Key elements of the enforcement wave include:

- Programmed inspections at manufacturing facilities identified through OSHA's Site-Specific Targeting program
- Emphasis on machine-specific energy control procedures — generic, one-size-fits-all LOTO programs will be cited
- Verification of annual periodic inspections of energy control procedures
- Review of authorized and affected employee training documentation

## Who Is Affected

Manufacturing employers in all sectors, with priority targeting of:

- Food and beverage processing
- Automotive parts manufacturing
- Metal fabrication and machining
- Packaging and bottling operations
- Plastics and rubber manufacturing

## What Employers Must Do

1. **Develop machine-specific energy control procedures.** Generic procedures that say "disconnect energy and lock out" are not compliant. Each machine with a potential for unexpected energization must have a documented, step-by-step procedure identifying all energy sources and the specific isolation methods.

2. **Conduct annual periodic inspections.** An authorized employee other than the one using the procedure must observe a representative sample of energy control procedures annually. Document the inspection — inspector name, employees observed, machines reviewed, date, and findings.

3. **Train and retrain employees.** Authorized employees must understand the specific energy control procedures for every machine they service. Affected employees must understand why they cannot operate equipment that is locked out. Training must be documented.

4. **Audit your LOTO hardware.** Locks, tags, and lockout devices must be standardized, durable, and individually identifiable. Shared locks between employees are a citation waiting to happen.

5. **Address group lockout/tagout.** When multiple employees service the same equipment, you need a procedure that ensures each employee applies their own lock and the equipment cannot be re-energized until every lock is removed.

## Effective Date

The enforcement initiative is active now. Programmed inspections under this initiative have begun.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

LOTO violations are frequently classified as serious ($16,550) or willful ($165,514). Because OSHA cites per-machine and per-element deficiencies separately, a single facility inspection can generate dozens of citation items. An employer missing machine-specific procedures for ten machines could face ten separate serious violations — $165,500 from one inspection.

Write the procedures. Train the employees. Conduct the inspections. Or pay OSHA to teach you why you should have.
