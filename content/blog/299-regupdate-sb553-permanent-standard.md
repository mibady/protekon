# SB 553 Permanent Standard Rulemaking Begins

Listen up. This is not a drill and it is not optional.

Cal/OSHA has officially kicked off the permanent standard rulemaking process for SB 553 — California's workplace violence prevention law. The temporary standard that went into effect in 2024 was just the appetizer. The main course is arriving, and it is going to have teeth.

## What Changed

The Standards Board voted to advance SB 553 from a temporary general industry safety order to a permanent standard. This means the Advisory Committee is now drafting language that will become the final, enforceable regulation. The public comment period opens in Q2 2026, and the final standard is expected by early 2027.

The permanent standard will almost certainly include stricter requirements around hazard assessment documentation, incident response protocols, and employee training frequency. Employers who barely squeaked by under the temporary standard are about to find out what "minimum viable compliance" actually costs.

## Who Is Affected

Every employer in California with one or more employees. Period. There is no industry exemption. There is no size exemption. If you have a W-2 employee in the state of California, this applies to you.

Healthcare, retail, hospitality, and social services employers should pay particular attention. The Advisory Committee has signaled that industry-specific appendices are under consideration — which means your sector may get additional requirements layered on top of the baseline.

## What Employers Must Do

**Right now — before the permanent standard drops:**

1. **Audit your existing WVPP.** If you wrote it in 2024 and shoved it in a drawer, pull it out. Is your hazard assessment current? Have you documented your periodic reviews? If the answer is no, you are already behind.

2. **Document your training.** The permanent standard will almost certainly require annual training with documented attendance. Start building that paper trail today. Not tomorrow. Today.

3. **Establish your incident log.** Every workplace violence incident — threats, physical altercations, near-misses — needs to be logged. Not in someone's head. Not in an email thread. In a formal, accessible log.

4. **Participate in the comment period.** When the draft language publishes, read it. Submit comments. Shape the regulation before it shapes you.

## Effective Date

The permanent standard is projected for adoption in Q1 2027, with a compliance deadline 90 days after publication. But the temporary standard remains fully enforceable right now. You cannot wait for the permanent version to start complying.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Cal/OSHA general violations start at $18,000 per instance. Serious violations run up to $25,000. Willful or repeat violations can hit $156,259 per instance. And those numbers adjust upward annually.

The math is simple. A Workplace Violence Prevention Plan through Protekon costs a fraction of a single citation. The fine for not having one costs more than most employers spend on safety in an entire year.

Stop gambling. Start complying.
