# FAQ: Is the WVPP the same as the IIPP?

No. They are separate documents, governed by separate laws, with separate requirements, and cited independently. You need both. One does not satisfy the other.

**IIPP — Injury and Illness Prevention Program:**
Required under 8 CCR 3203. This is California's comprehensive workplace safety program. Every employer must have one. It covers all workplace hazards — slips, falls, chemical exposure, ergonomics, machinery, electrical, everything. It has been required for decades.

**WVPP — Workplace Violence Prevention Plan:**
Required under Labor Code 6401.9 (SB 553), effective July 1, 2024. This specifically addresses workplace violence — threats, assaults, intimidation, and aggressive behavior. It has its own required elements that do not exist in the IIPP framework.

**Why you cannot combine them:**

The WVPP requires elements the IIPP does not:
- Four-type violence classification
- Violent incident log (separate from OSHA 300)
- Multi-employer coordination specific to violence
- Post-incident response procedures
- Emergency response protocols for active violence events
- Annual review specific to the violence prevention plan

The IIPP requires elements the WVPP does not:
- Scheduled periodic inspections
- Hazard assessment across all categories
- Accident/exposure investigation procedures
- Communication system for general safety

**How they get cited:**

Cal/OSHA can cite you for an IIPP deficiency and a WVPP deficiency in the same inspection. They are separate violations with separate penalties. Having an excellent IIPP does not protect you from a WVPP citation.

**The bottom line:** Two plans. Two documents. Two sets of training. Two review cycles. There is no shortcut here, and attempting to merge them into a single document creates gaps that inspectors are trained to find.

Build both. Maintain both. Train on both.
