title: "FAQ: How long must I keep safety records?"
faqCategory: cal-osha
excerpt: "Safety record retention varies: OSHA 300 logs for 5 years, training records for employment plus 3 years, WVPP incident logs for 5 years, and SDS records for 30 years if exposure occurred."
---

Record retention is one of those unglamorous compliance requirements that destroys companies when they get it wrong. Here are the numbers you need to know.

**OSHA 300 Logs (injury and illness records):** 5 years.

Maintain the OSHA 300 Log, 300A Summary, and 301 Incident Reports for five years following the end of the calendar year they cover. The 300A Summary must be posted in the workplace from February 1 through April 30 each year.

**Training records:** Duration of employment plus 3 years.

Keep records of all safety training -- sign-in sheets, topics covered, dates, trainer information -- for as long as the employee works for you, plus three years after separation. This applies to SB 553 training, IIPP training, hazard-specific training, and any other required safety training.

**WVPP incident log:** 5 years.

The workplace violence incident log required under SB 553 must be maintained for a minimum of five years. Every incident -- whether it resulted in injury or not -- must be documented and retained.

**Safety Data Sheets (SDS):** 30 years if exposure occurred.

If employees were exposed to hazardous chemicals, you must retain the SDS for 30 years. This is not a typo. Thirty years. Chemical exposure illnesses can take decades to manifest, and the SDS is evidence of what employees were exposed to and when.

**Hazard assessments and written programs:** Duration of the program plus updates.

Retain all versions of your IIPP, WVPP, and other written safety programs, including superseded versions, to demonstrate your compliance history.

**The simple rule:** When in doubt, keep it for five years minimum. For chemical exposure records, keep them for 30. Storage is cheap. Citations are not.
