# FAQ: What is the process for contesting an OSHA citation?

You received a citation. You disagree. Here is what you do — and the deadline that will end your options if you miss it.

**The 15-working-day deadline:**
You have 15 working days from receipt of the citation to file a notice of contest. Not calendar days — working days. But do not play games with the count. If you miss this deadline, the citation becomes a final order. No appeal. No negotiation. No second chances. The penalty is due and the abatement is mandatory.

**Option 1: Informal Conference (most common)**
Request an informal conference with the Cal/OSHA district manager. This is a negotiation, not a trial. You present your side, discuss the facts, and often reach a settlement. Most employers achieve a 30% to 50% reduction in penalties through informal conference. The citation classification may also be renegotiated.

This is the path most employers should take. It is faster, cheaper, and more predictable than a formal appeal.

**Option 2: Formal Appeal to OSHAB**
If informal conference does not resolve the issue, you can appeal to the Occupational Safety and Health Appeals Board. This is a formal administrative hearing with testimony, evidence, and legal arguments. You will need an attorney. The process takes months to over a year.

**Option 3: Settlement**
At any point during the appeal process, you can negotiate a settlement with Cal/OSHA. Most formal appeals settle before hearing.

**What you can contest:**
- Whether the violation occurred at all
- The citation classification (serious vs. general)
- The penalty amount
- The abatement deadline

**What you need to win:**
Documentation. Photographs. Training records. Maintenance logs. Written procedures. The employer with documentation wins. The employer without it pays.

Act within 15 working days. Everything else is optional. That deadline is not.
