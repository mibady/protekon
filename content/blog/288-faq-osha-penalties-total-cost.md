# FAQ: How much do OSHA penalties actually cost?

The penalty on the citation is the least of your problems. The real cost of an OSHA violation is 3x to 5x the fine itself. Most employers never see the full picture until the bills arrive.

**Direct penalty:** The number on the citation. Let's say $15,000 for a serious violation.

Now add the costs nobody warns you about:

**Legal fees:** Contesting a citation, negotiating at informal conference, or defending at a hearing requires legal counsel. $5,000 to $25,000 is common even for straightforward cases.

**Insurance premium increases:** Your workers' compensation carrier and general liability insurer both factor in OSHA citations. Expect premium increases of 20% to 40% following a serious citation. Across a multi-year policy period, this alone can dwarf the original fine.

**Workers' compensation experience modification rate:** A serious injury that triggered the inspection drives your EMR up. A higher EMR means higher premiums for 3 years. For a company paying $200,000 annually in workers' comp, a 0.15 EMR increase costs $30,000 per year — $90,000 over the modifier period.

**Lost productivity:** The inspection process disrupts operations. Employee interviews, document production, hazard abatement — all take time. Your crew is not working while they are talking to the inspector.

**Reputation damage:** In industries where safety records are checked during bidding (construction, manufacturing, government contracts), a citation history can disqualify you from work that would have been yours.

**The real math:**

A $15,000 citation routinely generates $50,000 to $75,000 in total cost when you factor in legal, insurance, productivity, and reputation impacts.

A $180,000 willful violation? That can be a quarter-million-dollar event.

Prevention is not a cost center. It is the cheapest insurance you will ever buy.
