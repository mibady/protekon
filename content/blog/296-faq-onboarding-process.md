# FAQ: What does the onboarding process look like?

Two to four weeks from kickoff to fully operational. Not months. Not "we will get back to you." A defined process with clear milestones and a specific end date.

**Week 1: Compliance Assessment**
We audit your current safety programs — or lack thereof. What do you have? What is missing? What is outdated? We review your IIPP, WVPP, training records, incident logs, and any existing programs. Most employers discover gaps they did not know existed. That is the point.

We also identify your NAICS code and vertical classification to determine exactly which Cal/OSHA standards apply to your operation.

**Week 2: Plan Development**
We build your customized compliance programs. Your IIPP is written for your specific hazards, your specific workplace, your specific operations — not a template with your company name pasted in. Your WVPP is developed with your input, addressing the violence types most relevant to your industry and workforce.

Every program is written in plain language. If your supervisors cannot understand it, it is useless.

**Week 3: Training Setup**
We configure your training program — topics, schedules, languages, delivery methods. Initial training sessions are scheduled for your team. Documentation templates are set up so every training event is recorded properly from day one.

**Week 4: Dashboard Activation**
Your compliance dashboard goes live. Training tracking, documentation management, compliance calendar, and — for Professional and Multi-Site tiers — enforcement intelligence. Everything in one place.

**What you provide:** Access to your current safety documents (if any), a walkthrough of your facility, and 2-3 hours of your time across the four weeks for interviews and review.

**What we deliver:** A fully built, inspection-ready compliance program with training infrastructure and ongoing management.

You do not need to be a safety expert. That is our job.
