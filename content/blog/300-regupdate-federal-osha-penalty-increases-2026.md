# 2026 Federal OSHA Penalty Increases

Every January, OSHA adjusts its maximum penalty amounts for inflation. Every January, employers act surprised. Stop being surprised.

## What Changed

Effective January 15, 2026, the Federal OSHA penalty structure increased across the board per the Federal Civil Penalties Inflation Adjustment Act:

- **Other-Than-Serious violations:** Up to $16,550 per violation (from $16,131)
- **Serious violations:** Up to $16,550 per violation (from $16,131)
- **Failure to Abate:** Up to $16,550 per day beyond the abatement date
- **Willful or Repeat violations:** Up to $165,514 per violation (from $161,323)

These are not theoretical numbers. OSHA issued over $250 million in penalties in fiscal year 2025. They are collecting, and they are not slowing down.

## Who Is Affected

Every employer subject to the OSH Act — which is nearly every private-sector employer in the United States. State-plan states (including California) typically match or exceed federal penalty levels, so do not assume your state exempts you from these increases.

## What Employers Must Do

1. **Update your risk calculations.** If your cost-benefit analysis for safety programs was built on last year's penalty numbers, it is already outdated. Recalculate with the new maximums.

2. **Review your OSHA 300 logs.** Incomplete or inaccurate recordkeeping is the easiest citation OSHA writes. Make sure your logs are current through Q1 2026.

3. **Address repeat violations immediately.** The jump from a serious violation ($16,550) to a repeat violation ($165,514) is a 10x multiplier. If you were cited for something in the last five years and have not fixed it, you are sitting on a financial time bomb.

4. **Train your supervisors.** Front-line supervisors who do not understand OSHA standards create OSHA citations. Invest in supervisor safety training now.

## Effective Date

January 15, 2026. These penalties apply to all citations issued after that date, regardless of when the violation occurred.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

See above. But here is the part most employers miss: OSHA can and does issue per-instance citations. Ten employees exposed to the same hazard can mean ten separate violations. A single willful finding with ten exposed workers is $1.65 million. One inspection. One hazard. Seven figures.

The cost of compliance is always cheaper than the cost of a citation. Always.
