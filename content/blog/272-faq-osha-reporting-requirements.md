title: "FAQ: What must I report to OSHA and when?"

Reporting timelines are non-negotiable. Miss them and you have a violation before anyone even looks at the underlying incident.

**Federal OSHA reporting requirements:**

- **Fatality:** Report within 8 hours of learning about it. Not 8 business hours. Eight hours, including nights and weekends.
- **In-patient hospitalization:** Report within 24 hours.
- **Amputation:** Report within 24 hours.
- **Loss of an eye:** Report within 24 hours.

Report by calling the nearest OSHA Area Office during business hours, or the 24-hour hotline at 1-800-321-OSHA (6742) any time.

**Cal/OSHA reporting requirements (California employers pay attention):**

California broadens the definition. You must report any **serious injury, illness, or death** to Cal/OSHA within 8 hours. Not 24. Eight.

Cal/OSHA defines "serious" more broadly than federal OSHA:
- Any injury or illness requiring inpatient hospitalization (not just overnight stays)
- Amputation
- Loss of an eye
- Serious degree of permanent disfigurement
- Any injury or illness where the employee is admitted to the hospital for more than 24 hours for treatment other than observation

**What the report must include:**

- Time and date of the incident
- Employer name, address, and contact
- Name and job title of the person reporting
- Address of the incident site
- Name of the injured employee
- Nature of the injury or illness
- Description of what happened
- Location where the incident occurred

**The consequences of not reporting:**

Failure to report is a separate, independent violation. Even if the underlying incident was truly an accident with no safety violations, failing to report it on time is its own citation. You can be compliant on every safety standard and still get cited for a late report.

Set up a reporting protocol now, before you need it.
