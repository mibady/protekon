title: "FAQ: Does HIPAA apply to my business?"
faqCategory: hipaa
excerpt: "HIPAA applies to covered entities and their business associates. If you handle protected health information in any form, you are likely covered -- including self-insured health plans."
---

HIPAA applicability is not as straightforward as most business owners think. The answer depends on what you do with health information, not what industry you are in.

**Covered entities (directly regulated):**

- **Health plans.** Health insurance companies, HMOs, employer-sponsored group health plans, government health programs. If you are a self-insured employer providing health benefits, your health plan is a covered entity.
- **Healthcare providers.** Any provider who transmits health information electronically in connection with covered transactions -- claims, eligibility inquiries, referrals.
- **Healthcare clearinghouses.** Entities that process health information between standard and nonstandard formats.

**Business associates (indirectly regulated, but equally liable):**

Any person or organization that performs functions or activities involving the use or disclosure of protected health information on behalf of a covered entity. This includes:
- Billing companies
- IT service providers who access systems containing PHI
- Cloud hosting providers storing PHI
- Attorneys, accountants, and consultants who receive PHI
- Shredding companies, records storage companies
- Any vendor that touches PHI in any form

**The self-insured health plan trap:**

This is where many employers get caught. If your company self-insures its employee health plan, that plan is a HIPAA covered entity. The company itself must comply with HIPAA's Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules with respect to the plan's health information. Most self-insured employers do not realize this until an audit or a breach.

**The test is simple:**

Do you create, receive, maintain, or transmit protected health information? If the answer is yes -- in any form, electronic, paper, or oral -- HIPAA likely applies to you. The penalties for non-compliance range from $100 to $50,000 per violation, with annual maximums of $1.5 million per violation category.

Do not guess. Get a definitive answer.
