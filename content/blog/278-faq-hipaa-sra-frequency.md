title: "FAQ: How often must I conduct a HIPAA Security Risk Assessment?"
faqCategory: hipaa
excerpt: "HIPAA sets no fixed frequency for Security Risk Assessments, but OCR expects them annually and after significant changes. Absence of a current SRA is the number one audit finding."
---

Here is what the regulation actually says: the HIPAA Security Rule requires covered entities and business associates to "conduct an accurate and thorough assessment of the potential risks and vulnerabilities to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronic protected health information." It does not specify a frequency.

Here is what that means in practice: you need to do it at least annually, and you need to do it after any significant change. OCR has made this expectation abundantly clear through enforcement actions.

**Why annually:**

The threat landscape changes. Your technology changes. Your workforce changes. Your vendors change. A risk assessment that is two years old does not reflect your current risk profile. OCR auditors know this, and "we did one three years ago" does not satisfy them.

**Additional triggers for a new or updated SRA:**

- New technology implementations (EHR systems, cloud migrations, new devices)
- Significant changes to physical facilities
- Security incidents or breaches
- Changes in business operations or organizational structure
- New vendor relationships involving ePHI
- Regulatory changes

**The number one audit finding:**

The absence of a current, thorough Security Risk Assessment is consistently the most frequently cited deficiency in OCR audits and enforcement actions. Not the most glamorous finding. Not the most technical. Simply not having done the assessment.

**What a proper SRA includes:**

- Inventory of all systems that create, receive, maintain, or transmit ePHI
- Identification of threats and vulnerabilities to each system
- Assessment of current security controls
- Determination of the likelihood and impact of potential risks
- Assignment of risk levels
- Documentation of remediation actions for identified risks

**The critical distinction:**

An SRA is not a checklist you complete and file. It is a process that produces a documented analysis, a risk register, and a remediation plan. If your "risk assessment" is a ten-question form someone filled out in twenty minutes, it will not survive OCR scrutiny.

Do it properly. Do it annually. Document everything.
