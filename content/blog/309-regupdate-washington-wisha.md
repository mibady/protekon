# Washington L&I Updates WISHA Standards

Washington State's Department of Labor and Industries has finalized updates to the Washington Industrial Safety and Health Act (WISHA) standards. These are not minor tweaks. Two of the changes carry significant compliance implications.

## What Changed

The L&I rulemaking package includes two major updates:

**1. Permanent Outdoor and Indoor Heat Exposure Rule (WAC 296-62-095 through 296-62-09560)**

Washington's emergency heat rules from 2021 are now permanent. The final standard establishes:

- Action level at 80 degrees for outdoor work
- Mandatory employer response at 90 degrees including shade, water, and paid cool-down rest
- High-heat procedures at 100 degrees including buddy systems and mandatory rest schedules
- Indoor provisions for environments not controlled by HVAC

**2. Healthcare Workplace Violence Prevention (WAC 296-800-180)**

New requirements for healthcare employers to:

- Conduct annual workplace violence hazard assessments
- Develop facility-specific violence prevention plans
- Provide de-escalation and self-defense training
- Report violent incidents to L&I within 72 hours
- Establish workplace violence prevention committees with employee representation

## Who Is Affected

- **Heat rules:** All Washington employers with outdoor or non-climate-controlled indoor workers
- **Violence prevention:** Hospitals, clinics, behavioral health facilities, long-term care, home health agencies, and emergency medical services operating in Washington State

## What Employers Must Do

**For heat exposure compliance:**

1. Develop a written outdoor heat exposure prevention plan with provisions for water, shade, acclimatization, and emergency response.
2. Train all outdoor workers and supervisors on heat illness prevention before exposure begins.
3. Implement monitoring procedures when temperatures are forecast to reach 80 degrees or above.
4. Establish high-heat procedures for days exceeding 100 degrees.

**For healthcare violence prevention:**

1. Conduct an annual workplace violence hazard assessment specific to each facility.
2. Develop a written violence prevention plan with input from frontline employees.
3. Provide annual training on violence prevention, de-escalation, and incident reporting.
4. Establish a violence prevention committee with equal employee representation.
5. Implement a 72-hour incident reporting protocol to L&I.

## Effective Date

Heat exposure rules: Enforceable now (permanent rule filed March 2026). Healthcare violence prevention: July 1, 2026.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Washington L&I penalties for serious violations can reach $70,000 per violation. Willful violations carry penalties up to $140,000. Washington is a state-plan state with enforcement authority independent of federal OSHA.
