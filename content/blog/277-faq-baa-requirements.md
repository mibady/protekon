title: "FAQ: What must a Business Associate Agreement include?"
faqCategory: hipaa
excerpt: "A BAA must include permitted PHI uses, safeguard requirements, breach notification obligations, subcontractor BAA requirements, and provisions for PHI return or destruction at termination."
---

A Business Associate Agreement is not optional paperwork. It is a legal requirement under HIPAA, and its absence is itself a violation -- regardless of whether a breach ever occurs.

**Required elements of a BAA:**

**1. Permitted uses and disclosures.**
The BAA must specify exactly what the business associate is allowed to do with PHI and what it is not. Vague language like "business associate may use PHI as needed" is insufficient. Be specific about the services being performed and the PHI involved.

**2. Safeguard requirements.**
The business associate must agree to use appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent unauthorized use or disclosure of PHI. This is not a suggestion -- it maps directly to the HIPAA Security Rule requirements.

**3. Breach notification obligations.**
The business associate must agree to report any breach of unsecured PHI to the covered entity without unreasonable delay. Best practice is to specify a timeframe -- 30 days or less from discovery. The covered entity's 60-day notification clock to individuals starts when the business associate discovers the breach, not when they report it.

**4. Subcontractor BAAs.**
If the business associate uses subcontractors who will access PHI, the business associate must enter into BAAs with those subcontractors. The chain of protection must be unbroken.

**5. Termination and PHI disposition.**
The BAA must address what happens to PHI when the agreement ends. Options are return or destruction of all PHI. If neither is feasible, the BAA must extend protections indefinitely.

**6. Individual rights support.**
The business associate must make PHI available to satisfy individuals' rights of access, amendment, and accounting of disclosures.

**The enforcement reality:**

OCR audits specifically check for BAAs. Not having one with every vendor who touches PHI is a finding. Having one that is missing required elements is also a finding. Template BAAs from the internet frequently omit required provisions.

Get it right. Get it in writing. Get it signed before PHI changes hands.
