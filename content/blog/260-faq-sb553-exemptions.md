title: "FAQ: What are the exemptions to SB 553?"
faqCategory: sb-553
excerpt: "SB 553 exemptions are narrow: teleworkers, healthcare under Section 3342, law enforcement, corrections, and equivalent CBA coverage. Small businesses are NOT exempt."
---

People love looking for exemptions. Let me save you some time -- the exemptions to SB 553 are narrow, specific, and probably do not apply to you.

**The five exemptions:**

1. **Teleworkers.** Employees who work from a location of their own choosing that is not controlled by the employer. Your remote workers who never come into a company-controlled space are exempt. But the moment they step into your office, warehouse, or job site, they are covered.

2. **Healthcare facilities under Cal/OSHA Section 3342.** These employers already operate under a stricter workplace violence prevention standard specific to healthcare settings. They do not need to double up with SB 553 -- their existing obligations exceed it.

3. **Law enforcement agencies.** Their operational requirements and existing training protocols place them outside the scope of SB 553.

4. **Corrections facilities.** Same rationale as law enforcement -- existing frameworks already address workplace violence in these environments.

5. **Equivalent CBA coverage.** If a collective bargaining agreement contains workplace violence prevention provisions that meet or exceed SB 553 requirements, those employees are exempt.

**The exemption that does NOT exist: small business.**

This is the single most dangerous misconception I encounter. There is no employee count minimum. There is no revenue threshold. There is no "we are too small for this to apply" exemption. A sole proprietor with one W-2 employee working on-site must comply.

Do not confuse "we have never had an incident" with "we are exempt." Those are completely different statements. The law does not care about your incident history. It cares about your plan.

If none of those five categories describes your operation, you are covered. Build the plan.
