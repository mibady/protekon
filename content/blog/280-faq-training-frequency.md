# FAQ: How often must safety training be conducted?

There is no single answer because California does not have a single rule. Each safety topic has its own training frequency requirement. Miss one and you have a citable violation.

Here is what the major programs actually require:

**IIPP (Injury & Illness Prevention Program):**
- Initial training when hired or assigned new tasks
- Retraining when new hazards are introduced
- Retraining when the program is updated

**WVPP (Workplace Violence Prevention Plan):**
- Initial training when plan is established
- Training for new hires
- Annual refresher training for all employees
- Additional training when the plan changes

**Hazard Communication (HazCom):**
- Initial training when hired
- Retraining when any new chemical hazard is introduced to the workplace

**Heat Illness Prevention:**
- Initial training before outdoor work begins
- Refresher before each heat season
- Supervisors need additional training on monitoring and response

**Forklift/Powered Industrial Trucks:**
- Initial certification training
- Performance evaluation every 3 years
- Immediate refresher after any incident, near-miss, or observed unsafe operation

**Lockout/Tagout:**
- Initial training by role (authorized, affected, other)
- Retraining when procedures change
- Annual inspection of energy control procedures

The pattern is clear: California expects initial training plus ongoing reinforcement tied to changes, incidents, and calendar cycles.

The employers who get burned are the ones who train once during onboarding and never revisit it. Cal/OSHA inspectors ask employees directly what training they received and when. If your people cannot answer, your documentation will not save you.

Build a training calendar. Automate the reminders. Track completion dates. This is not optional.
