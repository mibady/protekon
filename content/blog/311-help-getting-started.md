# Getting Started with Your Protekon Account

Welcome to Protekon. You just made the smartest compliance decision your business will make this year. Now let us make sure you get set up correctly so the platform starts working for you immediately.

This guide walks you through your first login, profile setup, adding your locations, inviting your team, and understanding what your dashboard is telling you.

## First Login

After purchasing your Protekon subscription, you received a welcome email with your login credentials. Here is how to get started:

1. **Navigate to app.protekon.com** and click "Sign In."
2. **Enter your email and temporary password** from the welcome email.
3. **Set your permanent password.** Choose something strong — you are storing compliance data here, not social media posts. Minimum 12 characters, mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
4. **Enable two-factor authentication.** Protekon supports authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy, Microsoft Authenticator). We strongly recommend enabling 2FA immediately. Your compliance data is sensitive.

Once logged in, the Setup Wizard launches automatically to guide you through initial configuration.

## Profile Setup

The Setup Wizard walks you through five configuration steps. Each one matters because the information you provide drives the compliance recommendations and plan generation that Protekon delivers.

**Step 1: Company Information**

Enter your legal business name, DBA (if applicable), EIN, and primary business address. Select your state of incorporation and states of operation. This determines which state-specific regulations apply to you.

**Step 2: Industry Vertical**

Select your primary industry from the dropdown. Protekon supports 18 industry verticals including healthcare, construction, manufacturing, hospitality, retail, education, and social services. Your industry selection determines which vertical-specific compliance programs (HIPAA, silica, heat illness, etc.) are recommended beyond the baseline IIPP and WVPP requirements.

**Step 3: Company Size and Structure**

Enter your total employee count, number of locations, and organizational structure (single location, multi-location, franchise). Employee count determines certain regulatory thresholds — some requirements kick in at 10, 20, 50, or 100 employees.

**Step 4: Responsible Persons**

Designate your primary compliance contact — the person responsible for maintaining your plans and responding to regulatory inquiries. Designate your training coordinator — the person who schedules and tracks employee training. You can assign the same person to both roles if your organization is small.

**Step 5: Review and Confirm**

Review all entered information for accuracy. Click "Complete Setup" to finalize your profile.

## Adding Locations

If you operate multiple locations, each one needs its own compliance profile because hazard assessments are site-specific.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Locations** from the left sidebar.
2. Click **"Add Location."**
3. Enter the location name, street address, city, state, ZIP code, and the number of employees at that location.
4. Select the location type (office, warehouse, retail, clinical, manufacturing floor, etc.).
5. Assign a site-specific compliance contact.
6. Click **"Save Location."**

Repeat for each location. Your compliance dashboard will display a summary across all locations and allow you to drill into location-specific details.

## Inviting Your Team

Compliance is not a solo sport. Protekon supports role-based access so your team can contribute without seeing information they should not.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Team Members.**
2. Click **"Invite Team Member."**
3. Enter their name, email address, and assign a role:
   - **Admin** — Full access to all features, settings, and billing.
   - **Compliance Manager** — Access to plans, assessments, training, and incidents. No billing access.
   - **Supervisor** — Access to training schedules, incident reporting, and team-specific dashboards.
   - **Employee** — Access to their own training records, incident reporting, and company policies.
4. Click **"Send Invitation."** The team member receives an email with login instructions.

## Understanding Your Dashboard

After setup, your dashboard shows your compliance posture at a glance. Here is what each section means:

- **Compliance Score** — Your overall compliance percentage based on completed assessments, active plans, training status, and incident response metrics. Aim for 90% or higher.
- **Enforcement Alerts** — Active regulatory updates and enforcement campaigns relevant to your industry and jurisdiction.
- **Upcoming Deadlines** — Training renewals, plan review dates, and regulatory filing deadlines.
- **Training Status** — Employee training completion rates with overdue training highlighted in red.
- **Document Library** — Quick access to your generated compliance plans and supporting documents.
- **Recent Incidents** — Latest reported incidents and their investigation status.

Your dashboard updates in real time as you and your team interact with the platform. The compliance score recalculates daily.
