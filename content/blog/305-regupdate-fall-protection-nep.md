# Construction Fall Protection NEP Renewed

Falls remain the number one cause of death in construction. They have held that title for over a decade. OSHA is tired of writing the same press releases, so they have renewed and expanded the National Emphasis Program for fall hazards.

## What Changed

OSHA has renewed its Fall Protection NEP with expanded scope and increased inspection targets. The renewed program includes:

- Increased programmed inspections at construction sites with workers at heights of six feet or more
- Expanded focus on residential construction, where fall protection violations are most prevalent
- New emphasis on scaffolding, ladder, and aerial lift compliance
- Coordination with state-plan states to align inspection priorities

The renewed NEP also introduces a referral mechanism where any OSHA inspection at a construction site — regardless of the original purpose — will include a fall hazard assessment.

## Who Is Affected

All construction employers with employees working at heights of six feet or more above a lower level. High-priority targets include:

- Residential roofers
- Framing contractors
- Scaffold erectors and users
- Steel erection companies
- Painting and exterior finishing contractors

## What Employers Must Do

1. **Conduct a fall hazard assessment for every job site.** Before work begins at any elevation, identify fall hazards and determine the appropriate protection method — guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems.

2. **Provide and enforce fall protection equipment use.** Having harnesses in the truck does not count. Employees must be wearing them, anchored to appropriate anchor points, at all times when exposed to fall hazards.

3. **Train employees on fall hazards.** Each employee exposed to fall hazards must be trained by a competent person to recognize fall hazards and understand the procedures for minimizing them. Retrain when conditions change.

4. **Designate a competent person on every site.** Someone on-site must be capable of identifying existing and predictable fall hazards and have the authority to take corrective measures.

5. **Inspect equipment before each use.** Personal fall arrest systems, guardrails, and safety nets must be inspected before each use and immediately after any event that could compromise their integrity.

## Effective Date

The renewed NEP is active now. Programmed inspections under the new targeting criteria have begun.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Fall protection violations consistently rank as OSHA's most-cited standard. Serious violations carry penalties up to $16,550. Willful violations — and OSHA frequently classifies fall protection failures as willful when an employer has been previously cited — can reach $165,514 per instance.

One fall can end a life. One citation can end a business. Neither outcome is acceptable.
