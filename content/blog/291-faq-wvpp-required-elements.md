# FAQ: What must be included in a Workplace Violence Prevention Plan?

California's SB 553 mandates a written Workplace Violence Prevention Plan for nearly every employer. This is not a template you download and file away. It is a comprehensive plan with 10 required elements, and Cal/OSHA will check each one.

**The 10 required elements:**

1. **Responsible persons** — Named individuals accountable for implementing the plan. Not "management." Specific people with specific authority.

2. **Employee involvement** — How employees participate in developing, implementing, and reviewing the plan. This is not optional — the law requires their input.

3. **Multi-employer coordination** — If you share a worksite with other employers, how do you coordinate prevention efforts? Staffing agencies, subcontractors, and co-tenants all factor in.

4. **Hazard identification and evaluation** — How you identify workplace violence hazards specific to your operation, including environmental risk factors and employee concerns.

5. **Hazard correction** — Procedures for timely correction of identified violence hazards.

6. **Reporting procedures** — How employees report violent incidents, threats, and concerns without fear of retaliation. Must be clearly communicated.

7. **Emergency response** — What to do during an active incident. Evacuation, lockdown, communication protocols, law enforcement coordination.

8. **Post-incident response** — Medical care, threat assessment, employee support, and debriefing procedures after an incident occurs.

9. **Training** — Initial training when the plan is established, annual refresher training, and additional training when the plan is modified. Must cover all plan elements.

10. **Annual review** — The plan must be reviewed at least annually, after any violent incident, and whenever a new hazard is identified.

**Critical distinction:** The WVPP is a separate document from your IIPP. You cannot fold workplace violence into your Injury and Illness Prevention Program and call it compliant. Different law, different requirements, cited independently.

Build the plan. Train your people. Review it annually. This is the new baseline.
