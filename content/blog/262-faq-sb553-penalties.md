title: "FAQ: What are the penalties for SB 553 violations?"
faqCategory: sb-553
excerpt: "SB 553 violations carry penalties from $18,000 for general violations up to $180,000 for willful violations, and they are cumulative."
---

The penalties for SB 553 violations follow the same enforcement framework as other Cal/OSHA standards. And they add up fast.

**Penalty tiers:**

- **General violation:** Up to $18,000 per violation. This is for conditions that violate a standard but are unlikely to cause death or serious harm. Missing documentation, incomplete plans, gaps in your incident log.

- **Serious violation:** Up to $25,000 per violation. A condition where there is a realistic possibility that death or serious harm could result, and the employer knew or should have known about the hazard.

- **Willful violation:** Up to $180,000 per violation. The employer intentionally and knowingly violated a standard, or was aware of a hazardous condition and made no reasonable effort to address it. This is where penalties become devastating.

- **Repeat violation:** Enhanced penalties when the same or substantially similar violation is found within a five-year period. Cal/OSHA does not forget.

**The critical detail most employers miss: penalties are cumulative.**

You do not get one citation for "not having a plan." You get cited for each element you are missing. No written plan -- citation. No hazard assessment -- citation. No training documentation -- citation. No incident log -- citation. Each one carries its own penalty.

A single inspection of a completely non-compliant employer could produce four or five separate citations. At serious-level penalties, that is over $100,000 before you have even hired a lawyer.

And here is the part that should keep you up at night: if a workplace violence incident occurs and you have no plan, the penalties escalate dramatically. Cal/OSHA will classify that as willful, and now you are looking at $180,000 per violation plus potential criminal referral.

The cost of compliance is a fraction of the cost of a single citation. Do the math.
