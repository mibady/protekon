# Scheduling and Managing Compliance Training

Training is where compliance lives or dies. You can have the most beautifully written WVPP in California, but if your employees have not been trained on it — and you cannot prove they were trained — your plan is a liability, not a shield.

Protekon's training module manages the entire lifecycle: identifying what training is required, scheduling sessions, sending invitations, tracking completion, and alerting you before certifications expire.

## The Training Matrix

Navigate to **Training > Training Matrix** from the left sidebar.

The training matrix is your master view of who needs what training and when. It cross-references:

- **Regulatory requirements** — Based on your industry vertical, active compliance plans, and applicable regulations
- **Employee roles** — Different roles have different training requirements (supervisors vs. employees, authorized vs. affected LOTO employees, etc.)
- **Training status** — Current, due within 30 days, due within 60 days, or overdue

The matrix displays as a grid with employees on the left and training types across the top. Each cell shows:

- **Green checkmark** — Training current
- **Yellow clock** — Training due within 60 days
- **Red exclamation** — Training overdue
- **Gray dash** — Training not required for this employee's role

Click any cell to see the employee's training history for that specific type, including dates, sessions attended, and certificates.

## Required Training Types

Protekon automatically identifies required training based on your active compliance plans:

| Training Type | Frequency | Who |
|--------------|-----------|-----|
| WVPP — Workplace Violence Prevention | Annual + new hire (within 90 days) | All employees |
| IIPP — Injury and Illness Prevention | Annual + new hire | All employees |
| Heat Illness Prevention | Annual + new hire (before outdoor work) | Outdoor workers + supervisors |
| HIPAA Security Awareness | Annual + new hire (within 30 days) | All workforce members with PHI access |
| Bloodborne Pathogens | Annual + new hire (before exposure) | Employees with occupational exposure |
| LOTO — Lockout/Tagout | Initial + when procedures change | Authorized and affected employees |
| Hazard Communication | Initial + new chemical introduction | All employees |
| Fall Protection | Initial + when conditions change | Employees exposed to fall hazards |
| Supervisor Safety | Initial + annual refresher | All supervisors |

Your specific training requirements depend on your industry and active programs. The training matrix reflects only what applies to you.

## Scheduling a Training Session

1. Navigate to **Training > Schedule Session.**
2. **Select training type** from the dropdown. Only training types relevant to your active programs appear.
3. **Set date and time.** Choose a date that allows at least two weeks for employee notifications.
4. **Set location.** On-site, off-site, or virtual. For virtual sessions, Protekon integrates with Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet — you can embed the meeting link directly.
5. **Select attendees.** You can select individual employees, entire departments, or use the "Overdue/Due Soon" filter to automatically select employees whose training is expiring.
6. **Assign trainer.** Enter the trainer's name, title, and qualifications. This information appears on training certificates and records.
7. **Add session details.** Duration, agenda, materials needed, any pre-session requirements.
8. **Click "Schedule."**

## Sending Invitations

After scheduling, Protekon sends automated invitations:

- **Email invitation** — Sent to each attendee with session details, date, time, location, and calendar attachment (.ics file)
- **Manager notification** — The attendee's direct supervisor receives a notification of the scheduled training
- **Dashboard reminder** — The training appears on each attendee's Protekon dashboard under "Upcoming Training"

You can customize the invitation template in **Settings > Notifications > Training Invitations.**

## Deadline Tracking

Protekon tracks training deadlines at three levels:

**Individual level:** Each employee's training due dates are visible in their profile and in the training matrix. Employees with the Employee role can see their own training status on their personal dashboard.

**Department level:** Supervisors see aggregate training compliance for their department, including the number of employees current, due soon, and overdue.

**Organization level:** Compliance Managers and Admins see organization-wide training compliance on the main dashboard, including training completion percentages and overdue counts by training type.

## Automated Reminders

Protekon sends automated reminders at configurable intervals:

- **60 days before expiration** — "Heads up" notification to the employee and training coordinator
- **30 days before expiration** — "Action needed" notification to the employee, supervisor, and training coordinator
- **14 days before expiration** — "Urgent" notification to all parties
- **On expiration date** — "Overdue" notification with the employee's compliance score impact noted
- **Weekly overdue digest** — Training coordinator receives a weekly summary of all overdue training

Customize reminder intervals in **Settings > Notifications > Training Reminders.** You can add or remove reminder touchpoints and modify the notification recipients.

## Tips for Effective Training Management

- **Batch by department.** Schedule training for entire departments at once to minimize operational disruption.
- **Stagger large teams.** If you have 50 or more employees, run multiple smaller sessions rather than one giant session. Smaller groups mean better engagement and easier documentation.
- **Schedule before the deadline.** Do not wait until training expires. Schedule renewal sessions 30 days before expiration to allow for rescheduling if something comes up.
- **Include supervisors.** Supervisors should attend the same training their employees attend, plus their own supervisor-specific training. They cannot enforce what they do not understand.
- **Document everything.** The next article covers recording training completion — the most critical step in the process.
