# Setting Up Your Workplace Violence Prevention Plan

Your Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP) is not a document you write once and file away. It is a living operational plan that describes how your organization identifies, prevents, and responds to workplace violence. California law requires every employer to have one. Protekon generates yours based on the specific information you provide about your workplace.

Here is how to set it up correctly.

## Before You Start

Make sure you have completed your Phase 1 Compliance Assessment. The WVPP setup process pulls from your assessment answers to pre-populate risk factors and recommendations. If you skipped the assessment, the setup wizard will prompt you to complete it first.

You will also need the following information ready:

- Names and titles of responsible persons (plan administrator, coordinator, emergency contacts)
- Physical layout details for each location (number of entrances and exits, public-facing areas, isolated work zones)
- Current security measures (cameras, alarms, access controls, security personnel)
- Reporting channels (how employees currently report concerns)
- Any history of workplace violence incidents (even informal reports)

## Step 1: Workplace Information

Navigate to **Compliance Plans > Workplace Violence Prevention Plan > Setup.**

The first section collects detailed information about your physical workplace:

**Facility Layout:**
- Number of entrances and exits
- Public-facing vs. employee-only areas
- Isolated work areas (stock rooms, parking structures, remote offices)
- Cash handling locations
- Areas where employees work alone or in small numbers

**Work Schedule:**
- Operating hours (early morning, late night, and weekend shifts carry higher risk)
- Number of employees per shift
- Positions that work alone or in isolated areas

**Current Security:**
- Physical security measures (locks, lighting, cameras, panic buttons)
- Access control systems (badge entry, visitor logs, reception screening)
- Security personnel (in-house, contracted, hours of coverage)
- Communication systems (intercoms, radios, emergency phones)

Fill out every field completely. The more specific your information, the more tailored your generated plan will be.

## Step 2: Hazard Assessment Questionnaire

This is the most important part of the WVPP setup. The hazard assessment identifies the specific workplace violence risk factors present in your environment.

Protekon walks you through a structured questionnaire covering four categories of workplace violence:

**Type 1 — Criminal Intent:** Violence by someone with no legitimate relationship to the business (robbery, trespass). Risk factors include cash handling, late-night operations, public accessibility.

**Type 2 — Customer/Client:** Violence by a customer, client, patient, or visitor. Risk factors include volatile populations, service denial situations, complaint handling.

**Type 3 — Worker-on-Worker:** Violence between employees. Risk factors include supervisory conflicts, terminations, disciplinary actions, interpersonal disputes.

**Type 4 — Personal Relationship:** Violence by someone with a personal relationship to an employee (domestic violence spillover). Risk factors include restraining orders, known threats, custody disputes.

For each category, rate the risk level (Low, Medium, High) and identify specific contributing factors present in your workplace. Be honest — an underestimated risk is a risk you are not prepared for.

## Step 3: Responsible Persons

Designate the individuals responsible for your WVPP:

- **Plan Administrator:** The person with overall responsibility for the WVPP. Typically the owner, HR director, or safety manager.
- **Site Coordinator(s):** One person per location responsible for day-to-day implementation.
- **Emergency Contacts:** Internal contacts for reporting threats or incidents.
- **External Resources:** Local law enforcement non-emergency numbers, employee assistance program contacts, crisis hotlines.

Each responsible person receives a notification and must acknowledge their role within the platform.

## Step 4: Reporting Channels

Define how employees report workplace violence concerns:

- **Primary channel:** How should employees report threats, incidents, or concerns? (Supervisor, HR, anonymous hotline, Protekon incident form)
- **Alternative channel:** If the primary contact is unavailable or is the subject of the concern, who does the employee contact?
- **Anonymous reporting:** Does your organization support anonymous reporting? Protekon includes a built-in anonymous incident reporting feature.
- **Non-retaliation statement:** Your WVPP must include a clear statement prohibiting retaliation against employees who report workplace violence concerns.

## Step 5: Review Your Generated Plan

After completing Steps 1 through 4, Protekon generates your WVPP. The generated plan includes:

- Purpose and scope
- Responsible persons and their duties
- Hazard assessment findings
- Hazard correction procedures
- Reporting procedures
- Emergency response procedures
- Post-incident response protocols
- Training requirements
- Recordkeeping procedures
- Plan review schedule

**Review every section.** The plan is generated from your inputs, but you know your workplace better than any algorithm. Edit any section that does not accurately reflect your operations by clicking the "Edit" button next to each section.

Once reviewed, click **"Activate Plan."** This makes the plan your official, active WVPP. It appears in your Document Library and is accessible to all team members based on their role permissions.

**Annual review reminder:** Protekon automatically schedules your annual plan review and notifies the Plan Administrator 30 days before the review date. Do not ignore this notification.
