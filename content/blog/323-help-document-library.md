# Navigating the Document Library

Your Document Library is where every compliance document lives — your plans, training records, incident reports, inspection checklists, and certificates. When an inspector walks through your door, this is what you pull up. When your insurance broker asks for your safety program, this is what you send.

Here is how to find what you need, when you need it.

## Accessing the Document Library

Navigate to **Documents** from the left sidebar. The Document Library opens to the main directory view, organized by category:

**Compliance Plans:**
- Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP)
- Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP)
- Vertical-Specific Programs (organized by program type)
- Emergency Action Plan

**Training:**
- Training Session Records (organized by date)
- Training Certificates (organized by employee)
- Training Materials Library

**Incidents:**
- Incident Reports (organized by date)
- Investigation Reports (organized by status: open, closed)
- OSHA 300 Log (by year)
- OSHA 300A Annual Summary (by year)
- OSHA 301 Incident Reports

**Inspections:**
- Inspection Checklists (organized by date and location)
- Hazard Correction Tracking Reports

**Policies:**
- Safety policies and procedures uploaded by your organization
- Regulatory reference documents

## Finding Your Plans

Your compliance plans are the most frequently accessed documents. Here is how to find them quickly:

**From the dashboard:** Click the document name in the "Document Library Quick Access" panel. This takes you directly to the current, active version.

**From the Document Library:** Click **Compliance Plans** and then select the specific plan. Each plan card shows:

- Plan name
- Version number (such as v3.2)
- Status: Active, Draft, Under Review, Archived
- Last modified date
- Last reviewed date
- Next review deadline

Only one version of each plan can be "Active" at a time. The active version is what Protekon considers your current, enforceable plan.

## Downloading Documents

To download any document:

1. Open the document.
2. Click the **"Download"** button in the top right.
3. Select format:
   - **PDF** — Formatted, printable version with your company header and Protekon branding
   - **Word (.docx)** — Editable version (for plans you want to customize further outside Protekon)
   - **Print** — Opens the browser print dialog for direct printing

For bulk downloads, navigate to the category view, check the boxes next to multiple documents, and click **"Download Selected."** Documents are bundled into a ZIP file.

## Viewing Revision History

Every document in Protekon maintains a complete revision history:

1. Open the document.
2. Click **"Version History"** in the document toolbar.
3. The version history panel shows:
   - Version number
   - Date of change
   - Who made the change
   - Summary of what changed (auto-generated from edit tracking)

4. Click any previous version to view it. Previous versions are read-only but can be downloaded.

5. To compare two versions, select two version checkboxes and click **"Compare."** Protekon highlights additions (green), deletions (red), and modifications (yellow) between the two versions.

Revision history is critical during inspections. If an inspector asks "when did you last update your WVPP?", you can show them the exact date, the specific changes made, and who authorized them.

## Sharing Documents with Employees

Employees need access to your compliance plans. California law requires that your IIPP and WVPP be accessible to all employees. Here is how to share:

**In-platform sharing:**
- Employees with a Protekon account automatically have access to active compliance plans through their Employee dashboard.
- They can view and download (but not edit) active plans.

**Email sharing:**

1. Open the document.
2. Click **"Share."**
3. Enter employee email addresses or select from your team list.
4. Choose sharing permissions: View Only or View and Download.
5. Add an optional message.
6. Click **"Send."**

The recipient receives an email with a secure link to the document. Links expire after 30 days (configurable in Settings).

**Physical posting:**
- For workplace postings, download the PDF version and print.
- Post in common areas accessible to all employees (break rooms, main hallways, near time clocks).
- Protekon's PDF format includes a footer with the version number and effective date, so employees can verify they are reading the current version.

## Building a Compliance Package

A compliance package bundles all your critical documents into a single, organized package — perfect for insurance renewals, new client onboarding (for contractors), or inspection preparation.

1. Navigate to **Documents > Compliance Package.**
2. Click **"Build New Package."**
3. Select which documents to include:
   - Active WVPP
   - Active IIPP
   - Active Vertical Programs
   - Current OSHA 300 Log
   - Recent OSHA 300A Summaries
   - Training completion summary
   - Incident summary (redacted)
   - Inspection records (last 12 months)
4. Set the package date range (for time-based documents like training and incident records).
5. Click **"Generate Package."**

The compliance package is generated as a single PDF with a table of contents, section dividers, and page numbers. It includes a cover page with your company information, the package date, and a compliance score summary.

Save your generated packages in the Document Library for future reference. Name them descriptively: "2026 Insurance Renewal Package" or "Q1 2026 Inspection Prep Package."

Your documents are only useful if you can find them. Protekon makes sure you always can.
