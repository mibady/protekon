# Setting Up Bilingual Training Programs

California's workforce speaks over 200 languages. Your compliance training needs to reach every employee in a language they understand — not because it is nice, but because the law requires it. Cal/OSHA mandates that safety training be provided in a language and vocabulary readily understood by employees. If your training is only in English and you have Spanish-speaking employees, you are not compliant.

Protekon supports bilingual and multilingual training programs. Here is how to configure them.

## Setting Employee Language Preferences

Before scheduling bilingual training, set language preferences for your employees:

1. Navigate to **Settings > Team Members.**
2. Click on any employee's profile.
3. Under **"Language Preference,"** select their primary language from the dropdown. Protekon currently supports English, Spanish, Chinese (Simplified and Traditional), Vietnamese, Korean, Tagalog, Armenian, Farsi, Hindi, and Punjabi.
4. You can also indicate the employee's English proficiency level: Fluent, Conversational, Limited, or None.
5. Click **"Save."**

When you set language preferences, the training matrix automatically flags employees who need bilingual training and ensures that session scheduling accounts for language needs.

## Accessing Translated Materials

Protekon provides translated training materials for core compliance programs:

**Available in Spanish and English:**
- WVPP training presentation and handouts
- IIPP training presentation and handouts
- Heat illness prevention training materials
- Hazard communication training materials
- Emergency action plan training materials

**Available in additional languages (varies by topic):**
- Heat illness prevention materials are available in all 10 supported languages (reflecting Cal/OSHA's emphasis on reaching agricultural and construction workers)
- WVPP and IIPP materials are available in English, Spanish, Chinese, Vietnamese, and Korean

To access translated materials:

1. Navigate to **Training > Materials Library.**
2. Use the language filter to select your desired language.
3. Preview, download, or attach materials directly to a scheduled training session.

**Custom materials:** If you need training materials in a language not currently supported, or you need to translate custom content specific to your workplace, Protekon offers a translation request service. Navigate to **Training > Materials Library > Request Translation** and submit your content. Turnaround is typically five to seven business days.

## Scheduling Bilingual Training Sessions

When scheduling a training session with bilingual requirements:

1. Navigate to **Training > Schedule Session.**
2. Select your training type and set the date, time, and location.
3. Under **"Language of Delivery,"** select the primary language and secondary language (if applicable).
4. Select attendees. The system highlights employees whose language preference matches the session language and flags those whose preference does not match.
5. **Best practice:** Schedule separate sessions for each language group when possible. Combined bilingual sessions work for small teams, but employees learn better in their primary language without the overhead of constant translation.

**Combined session options:**

- **Sequential delivery:** Present the material in English first, then repeat key points in the secondary language. This takes longer but ensures comprehension.
- **Parallel delivery with bilingual trainer:** A bilingual trainer alternates between languages throughout the session. Faster than sequential but requires a skilled bilingual presenter.
- **Primary language with translated materials:** Deliver the session in one language while providing printed materials in the other language for reference. This is the minimum acceptable approach.

## Documenting Language of Delivery

When recording session completion, Protekon requires you to document the language of delivery:

1. In the session completion form, confirm the **"Language(s) of Delivery"** field.
2. If a bilingual trainer was used, enter their name and document their language proficiency.
3. If translated materials were provided, confirm which materials were distributed in which languages.
4. Digital signatures from attendees include a language acknowledgment: "I confirm that I understood the training content as delivered."

This documentation is critical during inspections. Cal/OSHA inspectors routinely ask employees — in their primary language — whether they understood the training they received. If the employee says "no," your training documentation will not save you. Effective delivery matters as much as documentation.

## Maintaining Bilingual Records

All training records in Protekon are language-tagged:

- **Training certificates** are generated in the employee's primary language.
- **Session records** document the language of delivery.
- **Material records** note which language version was used.
- **Signature records** include the language acknowledgment.

When exporting training records for an inspection or audit, Protekon includes language documentation automatically. You do not need to compile this separately.

## Compliance Notes

- **Cal/OSHA requirement:** Training must be in a language "readily understood" by employees. This means the employee's primary language if they have limited English proficiency.
- **Written materials:** Safety information, hazard communication materials, and emergency procedures must be available in languages spoken by employees.
- **Signs and postings:** Required workplace postings (Cal/OSHA poster, heat illness poster, etc.) are available from Cal/OSHA in multiple languages. Post them in every language spoken by your workforce.

Language is not a barrier to compliance. It is part of compliance. Treat it that way.
