# Managing Your Vertical-Specific Compliance Programs

Your WVPP and IIPP are the baseline. Every California employer needs them. But depending on your industry, you likely have additional compliance programs that are equally mandatory and equally enforceable. Protekon calls these your vertical-specific compliance programs.

This guide covers how to view, manage, and maintain the vertical programs that apply to your business.

## What Are Vertical Programs?

Vertical programs are compliance requirements specific to your industry that go beyond the universal IIPP and WVPP. When you selected your industry vertical during account setup, Protekon identified which additional programs apply to you.

**Common vertical programs by industry:**

| Industry | Vertical Programs |
|----------|------------------|
| Healthcare | HIPAA Security Risk Analysis, Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure Control Plan, TB Exposure Control Plan, Patient Handling Program |
| Construction | Fall Protection Plan, Silica Exposure Control Plan, Excavation/Trenching Safety Plan, Crane Operation Program |
| Manufacturing | Lockout/Tagout Program, Machine Guarding Program, Hearing Conservation Program, Respiratory Protection Program |
| Restaurant/Hospitality | Heat Illness Prevention Plan (Indoor and Outdoor), Food Safety Program, Ergonomic Hazard Assessment |
| Dental | HIPAA SRA, Bloodborne Pathogen ECP, Radiation Safety Program, Infection Control Plan, Nitrous Oxide Monitoring |
| Agriculture | Heat Illness Prevention Plan, Pesticide Safety Program, Field Sanitation, Tractor/Equipment Operation |
| Warehousing | Powered Industrial Truck Program, Heat Illness Prevention (Indoor), Ergonomic Hazard Assessment, Dock Safety |

## Viewing Your Vertical Programs

Navigate to **Compliance Plans > Vertical Programs** from the left sidebar.

This page displays all vertical programs identified for your industry, organized into three categories:

- **Active** — Programs you have set up and activated. These contribute positively to your compliance score.
- **Required — Not Yet Set Up** — Programs that regulations require for your industry but you have not configured yet. These are pulling your compliance score down. Address them.
- **Recommended** — Best-practice programs that are not strictly mandatory but reduce your risk exposure. Setting these up earns bonus points on your compliance score.

Each program card shows:
- Program name and regulatory reference
- Status (Active, Draft, Not Started)
- Last review date
- Next review deadline
- Completion percentage (for programs in draft)

## Setting Up a Vertical Program

Click any "Not Yet Set Up" program to launch its configuration wizard. Each vertical program has its own setup flow tailored to its specific requirements, but they all follow a similar pattern:

1. **Workplace-specific inputs** — Information about your equipment, processes, materials, and exposures relevant to the program.
2. **Hazard data** — Specific hazard assessments related to the program (noise monitoring data for Hearing Conservation, exposure assessment for Silica, etc.).
3. **Responsible persons** — Individuals designated to implement and maintain the program.
4. **Control measures** — Engineering controls, administrative controls, and PPE specific to the program.
5. **Training requirements** — Program-specific training needs and schedules.
6. **Monitoring and review** — How you will monitor effectiveness and when you will review the program.

After completing the wizard, Protekon generates the program document. Review it, edit as needed, and click "Activate."

## Integration with Your Baseline Programs

Vertical programs do not operate in isolation. They integrate with your IIPP and WVPP:

- **Hazard assessment findings** from vertical programs automatically feed into your IIPP hazard assessment section.
- **Training requirements** from vertical programs appear in your unified training matrix alongside IIPP and WVPP training.
- **Incident types** related to vertical programs are available in your incident reporting form (a needlestick injury auto-links to your Bloodborne Pathogen ECP, for example).
- **Inspection checklists** from vertical programs integrate with your IIPP scheduled inspections.

This means you do not have to maintain separate tracking systems. Everything rolls up into your single compliance dashboard and unified compliance score.

## Updating Vertical Programs

Vertical programs need updates when:

- **Regulations change** — Protekon monitors regulatory updates and flags when a change affects one of your active programs. You receive an enforcement alert with guidance on what to update.
- **Your workplace changes** — New equipment, new processes, new chemicals, new locations. Any significant change should trigger a program review.
- **Annual review** — Every active program is scheduled for annual review. Protekon notifies the responsible person 30 days before the review date.

To update a program:
1. Navigate to the program in **Compliance Plans > Vertical Programs.**
2. Click **"Review/Update."**
3. Walk through the sections that need updating. Unchanged sections can be confirmed with a single click.
4. Click **"Save Updates"** to publish the revised version.

Protekon maintains full version history. Every previous version remains accessible in the Document Library for audit purposes.

## Adding Programs After Initial Setup

If your business evolves — new services, new equipment, new locations in different states — you may need additional vertical programs.

1. Navigate to **Compliance Plans > Vertical Programs.**
2. Click **"Add Program."**
3. Browse the program catalog or search for specific programs.
4. Select the program and follow the setup wizard.

You can also change your industry vertical in **Settings > Company Profile** if your business has fundamentally shifted. This triggers a reassessment of which vertical programs apply.

Keep your programs current. Keep your programs reviewed. Keep your programs integrated. That is how compliance works.
