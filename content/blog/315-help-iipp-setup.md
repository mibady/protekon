# Setting Up Your Injury and Illness Prevention Program

Every California employer is required to have a written, effective Injury and Illness Prevention Program. Not a template you downloaded from the internet. Not a binder your last safety consultant handed you in 2019. A current, site-specific program that addresses the eight elements required by Title 8, California Code of Regulations, Section 3203.

Protekon builds your IIPP based on the information you provide. Here is how to set it up right.

## The Eight Required Elements

Before we walk through the setup, understand what California requires. Your IIPP must address all eight elements:

1. **Responsibility** — Designated person with authority and responsibility for the program
2. **Compliance** — System to ensure employee compliance with safe work practices
3. **Communication** — System for communicating with employees about safety
4. **Hazard Assessment** — Procedures for identifying and evaluating workplace hazards
5. **Accident/Exposure Investigation** — Procedures for investigating workplace incidents
6. **Hazard Correction** — Procedures for timely correction of identified hazards
7. **Training and Instruction** — Training for new employees, new hazards, and refresher training
8. **Recordkeeping** — Documentation of program elements, inspections, training, and incidents

Protekon's setup wizard walks through each element in sequence.

## Step 1: Responsible Person

Navigate to **Compliance Plans > IIPP > Setup.**

Designate the individual with overall authority and responsibility for your IIPP:

- **Name and title** of the responsible person
- **Authority level** — This person must have the authority to implement safety decisions, allocate resources, and enforce compliance. If you designate someone without actual authority, your IIPP fails on Element 1.
- **Backup designee** — Who assumes responsibility when the primary person is absent?

The responsible person receives an in-platform notification and must acknowledge their designation.

## Step 2: Compliance System

Define how your organization ensures employees follow safe work practices:

- **Recognition programs** — How do you incentivize safe behavior? (Safety awards, recognition at meetings, performance evaluations)
- **Disciplinary procedures** — What is the progressive discipline process for safety violations? (Verbal warning, written warning, suspension, termination)
- **Observation methods** — How do supervisors monitor compliance? (Regular observations, spot checks, safety audits)

Document your actual practices, not aspirational ones. If you do not have a disciplinary procedure for safety violations, now is the time to create one.

## Step 3: Communication System

Describe how you communicate safety information to employees:

- **Safety meetings** — Do you hold regular safety meetings? How often? Who attends? Are minutes documented?
- **Safety bulletins and postings** — Where do you post safety information? How do you communicate urgent safety alerts?
- **Anonymous reporting** — Can employees report hazards anonymously without fear of retaliation? Protekon includes anonymous reporting functionality.
- **New employee orientation** — How are new employees informed about workplace hazards and safe practices on their first day?
- **Language considerations** — Do you have employees whose primary language is not English? How do you ensure safety communications reach them?

## Step 4: Hazard Assessment

This is the engine of your IIPP. Set up your hazard identification procedures:

**Scheduled Inspections:**
- Define your inspection schedule (weekly, monthly, quarterly) by location and area
- Assign inspectors (supervisors, safety committee members, or external consultants)
- Select the inspection checklist templates relevant to your industry (Protekon provides industry-specific templates)
- Set up inspection reminders in the platform

**Hazard Sources:**
- Review your Phase 1 and Phase 2 compliance assessment results — identified hazards carry forward automatically
- Enter any known hazards from OSHA 300 Log entries, workers compensation claims, or prior inspections
- Document hazards identified through employee reports

**Ongoing Identification:**
- New equipment or process hazard reviews
- Post-incident hazard assessments
- Employee-reported hazard investigation procedures

## Step 5: Investigation Procedures

Configure your incident investigation workflow:

- **Who investigates?** Designate individuals or teams responsible for investigating workplace injuries, illnesses, and near-misses
- **Investigation timeline** — Set your target timeline for initiating (24 hours) and completing (5 business days) investigations
- **Investigation method** — Protekon includes a guided investigation workflow with 5 Whys root cause analysis, corrective action planning, and follow-up tracking
- **Near-miss reporting** — Enable near-miss reporting and investigation. Near-misses are free lessons — they show you what almost went wrong before someone gets hurt

## Step 6: Hazard Correction

Define your process for correcting identified hazards:

- **Immediate hazards** — Imminent danger hazards must be corrected immediately or the area must be evacuated and barricaded
- **Non-immediate hazards** — Set correction timelines based on severity (24 hours, 7 days, 30 days)
- **Interim controls** — When permanent correction takes time, what interim protective measures will you implement?
- **Tracking** — Protekon tracks open hazard corrections with status, assigned person, and deadline

## Step 7: Training Configuration

Set up your training program within the IIPP:

- **New employee training** — What training must every new employee complete before beginning work?
- **Job-specific training** — What additional training is required for specific roles or hazard exposures?
- **Refresher training** — Set your annual refresher training schedule
- **Supervisor training** — Supervisors must understand their safety responsibilities and how to enforce them

Protekon's training module integrates with your IIPP to track completion automatically.

## Step 8: Recordkeeping

Configure your documentation retention:

- **Inspection records** — Retained for three years minimum
- **Training records** — Retained for three years minimum (we recommend five)
- **Incident investigation records** — Retained for five years minimum
- **Hazard correction records** — Retained for three years minimum

Protekon stores all records digitally with version history and audit trails. No filing cabinets required.
