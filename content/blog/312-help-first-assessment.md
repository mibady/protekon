# Completing Your First Compliance Assessment

Your Protekon account is set up. Your team is invited. Now comes the part that actually matters: figuring out where you stand. The compliance assessment is the foundation of everything Protekon does for you. Skip it or rush through it, and every recommendation you receive will be built on sand.

The assessment has two phases. Phase 1 covers platform-wide requirements that apply to every California employer. Phase 2 addresses vertical-specific requirements based on your industry. Both phases must be completed for Protekon to generate your compliance plans.

## Phase 1: Platform-Wide Assessment

Phase 1 covers the baseline compliance requirements that apply to all California employers regardless of industry. These include your Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP), Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP), hazard communication, emergency action plan, and recordkeeping.

**To start Phase 1:**

1. Navigate to **Assessments** from the left sidebar.
2. Click **"Start New Assessment"** or continue a saved assessment.
3. Select the location you are assessing. Multi-location employers complete a separate assessment for each site.

**Phase 1 question categories:**

**Workplace Violence Prevention (WVPP):** Do you have a written plan? When was it last reviewed? Do you have a designated person responsible for implementation? Have you conducted a hazard assessment? Do you maintain a violent incident log?

**Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP):** Do you have a written program with all eight required elements? Who is your designated responsible person? What is your hazard communication system? How do you conduct workplace inspections?

**Emergency Action Plan:** Do you have a written plan? Are evacuation routes posted? Have you conducted drills in the last 12 months?

**Hazard Communication:** Do you maintain a chemical inventory? Are Safety Data Sheets accessible to employees? Have employees been trained on hazardous materials in their work area?

**Recordkeeping:** Are you maintaining OSHA 300 Logs? Are you posting the annual 300A summary? Are records being retained for the required five-year period?

Answer each question honestly. The assessment is designed to identify gaps, not to make you look good. A gap identified here is a gap you can fix before an inspector finds it.

**Scoring:** Each question is weighted based on regulatory severity. Critical items (missing WVPP, no IIPP, no 300 Log) carry heavy penalties in the scoring algorithm. Your Phase 1 score appears on your dashboard immediately after completion.

## Phase 2: Vertical-Specific Assessment

Phase 2 activates based on the industry vertical you selected during setup. It covers requirements specific to your sector that go beyond the baseline.

**Examples by vertical:**

- **Healthcare:** HIPAA Security Risk Analysis, bloodborne pathogen exposure control, patient handling and ergonomics, workplace violence (enhanced requirements), sharps injury log
- **Construction:** Fall protection programs, silica exposure control, excavation and trenching procedures, scaffolding safety, crane and rigging
- **Manufacturing:** Lockout/Tagout procedures, machine guarding, powered industrial truck operations, noise exposure monitoring, respiratory protection
- **Restaurant and Hospitality:** Heat illness prevention (indoor and outdoor), slip/trip/fall prevention, chemical handling, ergonomics for repetitive tasks
- **Dental:** HIPAA SRA, bloodborne pathogen exposure control, radiation safety, nitrous oxide monitoring, infection control

**To complete Phase 2:**

1. After finishing Phase 1, the system automatically presents your vertical-specific assessment.
2. Answer each section thoroughly. These questions tend to be more technical and may require input from supervisors or department managers.
3. If you need to pause, click **"Save Progress."** You can return and continue from where you left off.

## Understanding Your Results

After completing both phases, Protekon generates your Gap Analysis Report:

- **Critical Gaps** (Red) — Missing required programs or plans. These represent immediate regulatory exposure and should be addressed first.
- **Significant Gaps** (Orange) — Incomplete programs or outdated documentation. These need attention within 30 days.
- **Minor Gaps** (Yellow) — Best-practice recommendations or documentation improvements. Address within 90 days.
- **Compliant** (Green) — Items where your current practices meet or exceed regulatory requirements.

The Gap Analysis Report feeds directly into Protekon's plan generation engine. When you set up your WVPP, IIPP, or vertical-specific programs, the system pre-populates recommendations based on your assessment answers.

## Tips for an Accurate Assessment

- **Be honest.** Inflating your answers helps no one. The assessment exists to protect you, not judge you.
- **Involve your supervisors.** Front-line managers often know the real answer better than the owner or compliance manager.
- **Assess each location separately.** A warehouse and an office in the same company have completely different hazard profiles.
- **Update quarterly.** Your initial assessment captures a point in time. Conditions change. Reassess at least quarterly or whenever significant changes occur.

Your assessment is complete. Now Protekon knows where you stand. Next step: Understanding Your Compliance Dashboard.
