title: "FAQ: How do I file a complaint with Cal/OSHA?"
faqCategory: cal-osha
excerpt: "File a Cal/OSHA complaint online, by phone, or in writing. Complaints can be anonymous. Retaliation against complainants is illegal under Labor Code 6310."

Filing a complaint with Cal/OSHA is straightforward, and employees need to know this because it is their right.

**Three ways to file:**

1. **Online.** Through the Cal/OSHA website. Fill out the complaint form, describe the hazard, identify the workplace. Takes about ten minutes.

2. **By phone.** Call your local Cal/OSHA district office during business hours. An intake officer will document the complaint. You can find district office numbers on the DIR website.

3. **In writing.** Mail or deliver a written complaint to the nearest Cal/OSHA district office. Include the employer name, worksite address, description of the hazard, and how many employees are affected.

**Anonymous complaints are accepted.**

You do not have to give your name. However, signed complaints carry more weight. A signed, written complaint from a current employee triggers a mandatory on-site inspection. An anonymous complaint may result in a phone inquiry to the employer first, rather than an immediate site visit.

**What happens after you file:**

Cal/OSHA evaluates the complaint for severity and urgency. Imminent danger complaints receive priority response -- potentially the same day. Other complaints are typically addressed within days to weeks depending on the backlog and hazard severity.

**Retaliation is illegal.**

California Labor Code Section 6310 explicitly prohibits retaliation against any employee who files a safety complaint, participates in an inspection, or exercises any right under occupational safety law. This includes termination, demotion, transfer, hours reduction, or any other adverse action.

If an employer retaliates, the employee can file a separate retaliation complaint. Cal/OSHA investigates these aggressively, and the penalties for retaliation are separate from and in addition to any safety citations.

Employers: your employees already have this right. Make sure they never need to use it.
