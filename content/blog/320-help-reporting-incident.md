# Reporting a Workplace Incident

Something happened at work. An injury. A near-miss. A threat. A violent incident. The clock is ticking, and what you do in the next few hours determines whether this becomes a documented, managed event or a compliance liability.

Protekon's incident reporting system captures every detail regulators require, triggers the right notifications, and starts the investigation workflow automatically.

## When to Report

Report every workplace incident. Period. This includes:

- **Injuries** — Any work-related injury, no matter how minor. A papercut today is a baseline for a workers comp claim next month.
- **Illnesses** — Work-related illnesses including heat illness, respiratory issues, repetitive strain, and occupational disease.
- **Near-misses** — Events that could have caused injury or illness but did not. These are free intelligence about hazards in your workplace.
- **Threats** — Verbal threats, written threats, intimidating behavior, or stalking related to the workplace.
- **Physical violence** — Any physical altercation, assault, or battery in the workplace.
- **Property damage** — Damage to equipment, facilities, or vehicles that indicates a safety hazard.

Do not filter. Do not decide that something is "too small" to report. Report it, and let the investigation determine significance.

## Filing an Incident Report

Any employee with a Protekon account can file an incident report. Here is how:

1. Navigate to **Incidents > Report Incident** or click the **"Report Incident"** button on the dashboard.
2. Alternatively, employees can access the incident form from the mobile-responsive web app at app.protekon.com/report from any smartphone.

### Required Fields

The incident form collects the following required information:

**When:**
- Date of incident
- Time of incident (approximate is acceptable)
- Shift (first, second, third, or specify)

**Where:**
- Location (select from your registered locations)
- Specific area within the location (loading dock, patient room 3, parking lot, etc.)

**Who:**
- Reporting person's name and role
- Injured or affected person's name and role (if different from reporter)
- Witnesses (names and contact information)

**What:**
- Incident type (injury, illness, near-miss, threat, violence, property damage)
- Description of what happened — be specific and factual. "Employee fell" is not helpful. "Employee slipped on wet floor in break room, fell and struck head on table edge" is helpful.
- Body part affected (if injury)
- Treatment received (first aid on-site, sent to clinic, emergency room, refused treatment)

**Contributing Factors:**
- Equipment involved
- Environmental conditions (wet floor, poor lighting, extreme heat, etc.)
- PPE in use at the time

### Attaching Photos

Click **"Add Photos"** to attach photographic evidence:

- Photograph the scene before anything is cleaned up or moved
- Photograph the specific hazard that contributed to the incident
- Photograph injuries (with the employee's consent)
- Photograph damaged equipment
- Photograph relevant signage or lack thereof

Photos are timestamped and geotagged (if the device allows) and stored securely within the incident record. You can attach up to 20 photos per incident.

## Supervisor Notifications

When an incident is submitted, Protekon triggers automatic notifications:

- **Immediate email and in-app notification** to the injured employee's direct supervisor
- **Immediate notification** to the site-specific compliance contact
- **Same-day notification** to the Plan Administrator and IIPP Responsible Person
- **Notification to HR** (if configured)

Supervisors receive a link to the incident report and are prompted to review the details and initiate the investigation workflow within 24 hours.

## Cal/OSHA Auto-Reporting Triggers

Certain incidents trigger mandatory reporting to Cal/OSHA. Protekon identifies these automatically:

**Immediate report required (call Cal/OSHA within 8 hours):**
- Fatality
- In-patient hospitalization
- Amputation
- Loss of an eye

**Report required within 24 hours:**
- Serious injury or illness (defined as any injury requiring treatment beyond first aid)

When you file an incident that meets these criteria, Protekon displays a prominent alert: **"Cal/OSHA REPORTING REQUIRED"** with the Cal/OSHA district office phone number and a checklist of information you need to provide in your call.

Protekon does not make the call for you — that must be done by a live person. But the platform ensures you know the call is required, provides the number, and logs when the call was made.

## OSHA 300 Log Auto-Population

When a recordable incident is filed, Protekon automatically populates the relevant entry on your OSHA 300 Log:

- Case number (auto-generated)
- Employee name and job title
- Date of injury or illness
- Where the event occurred
- Description of injury or illness
- Classification (death, days away from work, job transfer or restriction, other recordable)

You can review and edit the 300 Log entry from **Documents > OSHA 300 Log.** Protekon flags entries that may need additional information or reclassification.

## Anonymous Reporting

Protekon supports anonymous incident reporting for situations where the reporter fears retaliation:

- Navigate to app.protekon.com/report/anonymous
- No login required
- The report captures the same incident details but without identifying the reporter
- Anonymous reports are routed to the Plan Administrator and Compliance Manager only

Encourage anonymous reporting. The alternative is unreported incidents — which are far more dangerous than uncomfortable conversations.

Report early. Report completely. Let the investigation do the rest.
