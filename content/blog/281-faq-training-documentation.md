# FAQ: What must training documentation include?

If you cannot prove the training happened, it did not happen. That is how Cal/OSHA sees it, and frankly, they are right.

Your training documentation must include all of the following:

- **Date of training** — the specific calendar date, not "Q3 2025"
- **Time and duration** — start time, end time, total hours
- **Specific topics covered** — detailed descriptions, not "safety meeting" or "general training"
- **Trainer name and qualifications** — who delivered it and why they were qualified to do so
- **Attendee names and signatures** — every person who participated, with their signature confirming attendance
- **Training method** — classroom, hands-on, online, toolbox talk, or combination
- **Language of delivery** — critical if you have a multilingual workforce

Here is where most employers fail: they write "Safety Meeting" on a sign-in sheet and call it documentation. An inspector sees that and immediately knows your program is weak. What safety topic? What was discussed? Who taught it? None of those questions are answered.

**What good documentation looks like:**

"June 12, 2025, 7:00-8:30 AM. Heat Illness Prevention refresher training. Covered recognition of heat-related symptoms, water-rest-shade requirements, emergency response procedures, and high-heat protocols above 95 degrees. Delivered in English and Spanish by Maria Rodriguez, Certified Safety Professional. 14 attendees — see attached sign-in sheet with signatures."

That is documentation that survives an inspection.

Keep records for at least 5 years. Store them where you can find them in under 5 minutes. When an inspector asks, hesitation looks like negligence.

Document like someone is going to audit you — because they will.
