# Running Your Annual Compliance Review

California requires that your IIPP be reviewed at least annually. Your WVPP requires periodic review and must be updated when conditions change. Your vertical programs have their own review cycles. An annual compliance review ensures all your programs are current, accurate, and enforceable.

This is not a box-checking exercise. This is the process that keeps your compliance program alive. Protekon walks you through it step by step.

## When to Run Your Annual Review

Protekon schedules your annual review based on the activation date of each compliance plan. You receive reminders:

- **60 days before** — "Annual review approaching" notification to the Plan Administrator
- **30 days before** — "Annual review due soon" notification to the Plan Administrator and Compliance Manager
- **On the review date** — "Annual review due today" notification to all responsible persons
- **Overdue** — Weekly reminders until the review is completed

You can also trigger a review manually at any time. Reasons to review outside the annual cycle:

- Significant workplace changes (new location, new equipment, new processes)
- Change in responsible persons (someone leaves the company or changes roles)
- Regulatory changes affecting your programs
- After a significant workplace incident
- After a Cal/OSHA inspection

## Triggering the Annual Review

1. Navigate to **Compliance Plans > Annual Review.**
2. Click **"Start Annual Review."**
3. Select which plans to include in this review cycle:
   - WVPP
   - IIPP
   - Vertical-Specific Programs (select individually or "All Active")
4. Click **"Begin Review."**

Protekon opens the review workflow — a structured walkthrough of each selected plan with prompts at every decision point.

## Step 1: Regulatory Change Review

Protekon has been tracking regulatory changes throughout the year. The first step surfaces every regulatory update that affects your active plans:

**For each flagged change, you see:**
- The regulation that changed
- What changed (summary)
- Which section(s) of your plan are affected
- Recommended updates

**Your options for each flagged change:**
- **"Update Plan"** — Opens the affected section for editing. Make the necessary changes and save.
- **"No Change Needed"** — The regulatory change does not materially affect your plan. Document your reasoning.
- **"Flag for Further Review"** — You need more information or want to consult with legal counsel before deciding.

This step ensures your plans reflect current law — not the law as it existed when you first created them.

## Step 2: Workplace Change Review

Protekon prompts you to review whether your workplace has changed in ways that affect your compliance programs:

**Physical changes:**
- New locations added or locations closed?
- Renovations or layout changes at existing locations?
- New equipment installed or old equipment decommissioned?

**Workforce changes:**
- Significant changes in employee count?
- New job roles or responsibilities?
- Changes in work schedules or shift patterns?
- Changes in the languages spoken by your workforce?

**Operational changes:**
- New services or products offered?
- New materials or chemicals introduced?
- Changes in client or customer populations?
- Changes in security arrangements?

For each "yes" answer, Protekon guides you to the specific plan section(s) that need updating.

## Step 3: Update Responsible Persons

Review every designated responsible person across all your plans:

- **Plan Administrator** — Still employed? Still in the right role? Still has authority to implement?
- **Site Coordinators** — Still at their assigned locations? Still appropriate for the role?
- **Training Coordinator** — Still managing training? Still has bandwidth?
- **Emergency Contacts** — Contact information still current?

For any person who has changed, click **"Update"** and enter the new designee's information. The new person receives a notification and must acknowledge their designation.

This step catches the silent compliance killer: a WVPP that lists someone as the responsible person who left the company eight months ago.

## Step 4: Employee Input

California law encourages (and for some programs requires) employee participation in the safety program review process. Protekon facilitates this:

**Option A — Employee Survey:**

1. Click **"Send Employee Survey."**
2. Protekon sends a short survey to all employees (or a selected group) asking:
   - Do you know where to find the company's safety plans?
   - Do you know how to report a workplace hazard or incident?
   - Have you observed any workplace hazards that have not been addressed?
   - Do you have suggestions for improving workplace safety?
3. Survey responses are anonymous by default.
4. Results are compiled and included in the review record.

**Option B — Safety Committee Meeting:**

1. If you have a safety committee, schedule a review meeting.
2. Use Protekon's committee meeting template to structure the discussion.
3. Document meeting minutes, attendees, and any recommendations.
4. Upload the meeting record to the review.

**Option C — Individual Feedback:**

1. Collect feedback from individual employees through one-on-one conversations with supervisors.
2. Document feedback themes and specific concerns.

Employee input is not optional for your IIPP. Section 3203(a)(3) requires a system for communicating with employees about safety "in a form readily understandable by all affected employees." The annual review is the perfect time to verify that system is working.

## Step 5: Certification

After completing all review steps, Protekon presents a certification page:

**Review Summary:**
- Plans reviewed (list with version numbers)
- Regulatory changes addressed (count and details)
- Workplace changes addressed (count and details)
- Responsible person updates made (count and details)
- Employee input collected (method and participation count)

**Certification Statement:**

"I certify that I have reviewed the above compliance plans, addressed identified changes, updated responsible persons as needed, and collected employee input. These plans accurately reflect the current workplace conditions and regulatory requirements as of this date."

- Enter the certifying person's name and title (typically the Plan Administrator)
- Digital signature
- Date

Click **"Complete Review and Certify."**

## The Review Record

Upon certification, Protekon creates a permanent Review Record that documents:

- Date of review
- Plans reviewed
- Regulatory changes identified and how each was addressed
- Workplace changes identified and how each was addressed
- Responsible person updates
- Employee input summary
- Certification with signature

The Review Record is stored in **Documents > Annual Reviews** and is linked to each reviewed plan's version history. It is a permanent, immutable record that you reviewed your programs, took action on identified changes, and certified the results.

When an inspector asks "when did you last review your IIPP?", you pull up the Review Record. Date. Changes. Certification. Done.

Your annual review is the backbone of a living compliance program. It is the difference between a plan that protects you and a plan that collects dust. Protekon makes the process structured, thorough, and documented.
