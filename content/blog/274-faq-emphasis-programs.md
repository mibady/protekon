Emphasis programs are how OSHA and Cal/OSHA concentrate their enforcement resources. If your industry or hazard profile falls under an active emphasis program, your probability of inspection just went up significantly.

**Two types of emphasis programs:**

**National Emphasis Programs (NEPs).** Federal OSHA directs nationwide enforcement attention to specific hazards. Current and recent NEPs have targeted fall hazards in construction, combustible dust, lead exposure, trenching and excavation, heat illness, and process safety management. These programs apply across all federal OSHA states and often influence state plan states to adopt similar priorities.

**Local Emphasis Programs (LEPs).** Individual OSHA area offices and state plan agencies create targeted enforcement programs for hazards or industries relevant to their region. Cal/OSHA runs LEPs focused on California-specific concerns -- heat illness in agriculture, refinery safety, high-injury industries based on workers' compensation data.

**How they affect your business:**

The standards do not change. You are not held to a higher standard because of an emphasis program. But the enforcement intensity changes dramatically:

- **More programmed inspections.** Your establishment may be selected for inspection based solely on your industry code, without any complaint or incident.
- **Deeper inspections.** When inspectors arrive under an emphasis program, they are looking specifically for the targeted hazard. They know exactly what to check and where to find violations.
- **Less discretion.** Inspectors operating under emphasis programs have less flexibility to issue warnings instead of citations. The program exists to find and cite violations.

**What to do about it:**

Check the current active NEPs on OSHA's website and LEPs on Cal/OSHA's website. If your industry or hazard profile appears on either list, assume you will be inspected and prepare accordingly. Conduct an internal audit targeting exactly what the emphasis program targets. Fix what you find before an inspector finds it for you.

Emphasis programs are the government telling you where they are looking. Pay attention.
