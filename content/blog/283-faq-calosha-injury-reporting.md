# FAQ: When must I report an injury to Cal/OSHA?

You have 8 hours. That is it. Eight hours from the time you know about a serious injury, serious illness, or death to pick up the phone and report it to your nearest Cal/OSHA district office.

Not 8 business hours. Eight hours. If someone is seriously injured at 10 PM on a Saturday, you report it by 6 AM Sunday.

**What qualifies as reportable:**

- **Death** — any work-related fatality
- **Hospitalization for more than 24 hours** — for treatment, not just observation
- **Amputation** — loss of any body part
- **Loss of an eye**
- **Serious disfigurement**

California defines "serious" more broadly than federal OSHA. Do not use the federal standard and assume you are covered. If there is any question about whether an injury qualifies, report it. Over-reporting carries zero penalty. Under-reporting carries massive consequences.

**How to report:**

Call your nearest Cal/OSHA district office by phone. Not email. Not a form submission. A phone call. After hours, leave a message — but follow up first thing the next business day to confirm receipt.

**What to have ready when you call:**

- Your business name and address
- Time and date of the incident
- Location where it occurred
- Name and job title of the injured worker
- Brief description of what happened
- Nature and severity of the injury
- Name of your contact person

**What happens if you miss the deadline:**

Failure to report is a separate citable violation with its own penalties — up to $7,000 per incident. And it signals to investigators that your entire safety program may be deficient, which guarantees a deeper inspection.

The 8-hour clock starts when you know. Not when you investigate. Not when you fill out paperwork. When you know.

Report first. Investigate second.
