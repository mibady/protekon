# FAQ: How do I know which vertical my business falls under?

Your NAICS code determines your vertical. We handle the classification — you do not need to figure this out yourself.

**How it works:**

Every business has a NAICS code (North American Industry Classification System) that categorizes its primary activity. This code determines which Cal/OSHA standards apply to your operation. A restaurant faces different regulatory requirements than a roofing contractor, and both face different requirements than a medical office.

Protekon organizes California industries into 27 verticals across 3 tiers based on regulatory complexity and enforcement intensity.

**What the vertical determines:**

All businesses get the 8 baseline compliance templates — IIPP, WVPP, Heat Illness Prevention, HazCom, Emergency Action Plan, and the other foundational programs. These apply regardless of industry.

Your vertical determines which additional programs your operation requires. Construction needs fall protection, scaffolding, trenching, and silica exposure programs. Healthcare needs bloodborne pathogens, ergonomics, and patient handling. Manufacturing needs lockout/tagout, machine guarding, and confined space.

**How we classify you:**

During onboarding, we confirm your NAICS code and map it to the appropriate vertical. If your business spans multiple codes — say you have a warehouse and a retail storefront — we account for all applicable standards.

This is not guesswork. It is a systematic classification based on the same codes Cal/OSHA uses to target inspections. We know which industries are getting inspected most frequently because we monitor the enforcement data.

**The outcome:** You get exactly the compliance programs your specific business requires. Nothing missing that could result in a citation. Nothing extraneous that wastes your time.

We confirm the classification during onboarding. If it is wrong, we adjust. No additional charge.
