# HIPAA Enforcement Priorities for 2026

The HHS Office for Civil Rights has published its 2026 enforcement priority guidance, and the message is unmistakable: if you cannot produce a current, comprehensive risk analysis on demand, you will be fined. No exceptions. No excuses.

## What Changed

OCR's 2026 enforcement priorities target three areas with increased scrutiny:

1. **Security Risk Analysis (SRA) completeness.** OCR has found that over 80% of investigated breaches involve incomplete or outdated risk analyses. They are done tolerating checkbox compliance. Your SRA must be thorough, asset-specific, and current.

2. **Ransomware and cybersecurity readiness.** Following a record year of healthcare ransomware attacks, OCR is prioritizing investigations into whether covered entities had adequate safeguards — encryption, access controls, backup procedures, and incident response plans — before a breach occurred.

3. **Business Associate oversight.** Covered entities that cannot demonstrate active management of their Business Associate Agreements and vendor security practices will face increased scrutiny.

## Who Is Affected

Every HIPAA-covered entity and business associate:

- Hospitals and health systems
- Physician and dental practices
- Behavioral health and substance abuse treatment facilities
- Pharmacies
- Health plans
- Healthcare clearinghouses
- Any business associate handling PHI

## What Employers Must Do

1. **Complete a thorough Security Risk Analysis.** Not a questionnaire. Not a checklist someone downloaded from the internet. A comprehensive, asset-by-asset analysis of threats, vulnerabilities, and risk levels for every system that touches PHI.

2. **Document your risk management plan.** Identifying risks is step one. Documenting how you are mitigating each identified risk is step two. OCR wants to see both.

3. **Test your incident response plan.** Run a tabletop exercise for a ransomware scenario. Document the exercise, participants, findings, and corrective actions. OCR will ask for this documentation.

4. **Audit your Business Associate Agreements.** Confirm every vendor with PHI access has a current, signed BAA. Verify that your agreements include breach notification requirements and security obligations.

5. **Encrypt PHI at rest and in transit.** Encryption is an addressable safeguard under the Security Rule, but failing to implement it without a documented, defensible alternative is a recipe for a finding.

## Effective Date

These enforcement priorities are active now and will guide OCR's investigation and audit activities throughout 2026.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

HIPAA civil monetary penalties range from $141 to $2,134,831 per violation, with a calendar-year cap of $2,134,831 per identical provision. Criminal penalties can reach $250,000 plus imprisonment. And the reputational damage from a publicized breach settlement is incalculable.

A proper SRA costs thousands. A HIPAA settlement costs millions. Do the math.
