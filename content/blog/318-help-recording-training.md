# Recording Training Completion and Attendance

You held the training. People showed up. Now you need to prove it happened, prove who was there, and prove what was covered. This is where most employers fail. Not because they did not train — but because they cannot produce the records when an inspector asks for them.

Protekon makes recording training completion straightforward and inspection-ready.

## Recording a Completed Session

After a training session concludes, navigate to **Training > Sessions > [Your Session]** and click **"Record Completion."**

The completion form captures everything a regulator or inspector will ask for:

### Session Details

- **Actual date and time** — Confirm or adjust the scheduled date and time to reflect when the session actually occurred.
- **Actual duration** — How long was the session? Some regulations specify minimum training durations.
- **Location** — Where was the training conducted? (Address for in-person, platform for virtual)
- **Trainer name and qualifications** — Who delivered the training? What qualifies them to deliver it?
- **Training method** — Classroom, hands-on, virtual, self-paced, or combination.

### Topics Covered

Protekon pre-populates the required topics based on the training type. Check each topic that was covered during the session:

For example, a WVPP training session would include:

- Workplace violence policy overview
- Types of workplace violence
- Risk factors specific to this workplace
- Reporting procedures
- Emergency response procedures
- De-escalation techniques
- Post-incident procedures
- Employee rights and non-retaliation

If you covered additional topics beyond the minimum requirements, you can add them. More is better for your records.

### Materials Used

Upload the training materials used during the session:

- **Presentations** — PowerPoint, PDF, or Google Slides files
- **Handouts** — Any printed materials distributed to attendees
- **Videos** — Training videos shown during the session (upload files or paste links)
- **Assessments** — Quizzes, tests, or knowledge checks administered during or after training

Storing materials with the session record proves what content was delivered, not just that a meeting occurred. Inspectors love documentation. Give them documentation.

## Collecting Digital Signatures

Protekon includes a built-in digital signature feature for training attendance verification:

1. After entering session details, click **"Collect Signatures."**
2. **Option A — In-Person Collection:** Display the signature pad on a tablet or laptop. Pass the device to each attendee to sign with their finger or stylus. Each signature is timestamped and linked to the employee's profile.
3. **Option B — Remote Collection:** Click "Send Signature Request" to email each attendee a signature link. They sign electronically from their own device. Set a deadline for signature completion (we recommend 24 hours).
4. **Option C — Manual Upload:** If you collected physical signatures on a paper sign-in sheet, scan or photograph the sheet and upload it to the session record.

Digital signatures carry the same legal weight as wet signatures for training documentation purposes. Each signature record includes the employee's name, timestamp, IP address (for remote signatures), and a tamper-evident hash.

## Handling Absent Employees

Employees who miss scheduled training need to be tracked and rescheduled:

1. In the session completion form, mark absent employees by unchecking their attendance box.
2. Select a reason for absence (if known): illness, vacation, scheduling conflict, no-show, or other.
3. Click **"Schedule Makeup"** to create a makeup session for absent employees.
4. Alternatively, click **"Add to Next Session"** to automatically include them in the next scheduled session of the same training type.

Absent employees remain flagged as "training due" in the training matrix until they complete a makeup session. Their training deadline does not extend because they missed a session — the clock keeps ticking.

**Document the absence.** If an inspector asks why Employee X was not trained, "they missed the session" is not sufficient. "They were absent on this date, a makeup session was scheduled for this date, and they completed training on this date" is sufficient.

## Generating Training Certificates

After recording completion, Protekon generates individual training certificates for each attendee:

1. Navigate to the completed session and click **"Generate Certificates."**
2. Select which attendees should receive certificates (default: all who signed).
3. Customize the certificate if desired (your company logo, additional text).
4. Click **"Generate."**

Each certificate includes:

- Employee name
- Training type and title
- Date of completion
- Duration
- Trainer name and qualifications
- Topics covered (summary)
- Certificate number (unique, verifiable)
- Expiration date (based on renewal frequency)

Certificates are automatically stored in each employee's training record and in the Document Library. Employees with the Employee role can access and download their own certificates from their personal dashboard.

## Training Records Retention

Protekon retains all training records — session details, attendance, signatures, materials, and certificates — for the duration of your subscription plus three years. California regulations require a minimum three-year retention period for most training records. We recommend keeping them for at least five years.

All records are exportable. You can export individual employee training histories, session records, or your complete training archive at any time from **Training > Export.**

Your training only counts if you can prove it happened. Protekon makes proof automatic.
