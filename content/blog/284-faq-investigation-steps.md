# FAQ: What are the steps for investigating a workplace incident?

An incident happened. People are watching how you respond. Here is the process that separates competent operations from negligent ones.

**Step 1: Secure the scene and provide care.**
Medical attention comes first. Always. Then secure the area to preserve evidence and prevent additional injuries. Do not clean up, move equipment, or "get things running again" until the investigation is complete.

**Step 2: Gather facts immediately.**
Interview witnesses while memories are fresh — ideally within hours, not days. Take photographs of the scene, equipment positions, environmental conditions. Collect physical evidence. Document everything in writing.

**Step 3: Conduct root cause analysis.**
Use the 5 Whys method. The surface cause is almost never the real cause. "He slipped" is not a root cause. Why did he slip? Wet floor. Why was the floor wet? Leaking pipe. Why was it leaking? No preventive maintenance schedule. Now you have something actionable.

**Step 4: Develop corrective actions using the hierarchy of controls.**
Elimination first, then substitution, engineering controls, administrative controls, and PPE as a last resort. "Retrain the employee" is the weakest corrective action. Change the system, not just the behavior.

**Step 5: Document everything.**
Write a formal investigation report including: what happened, when, where, who was involved, root causes identified, and corrective actions planned.

**Step 6: Assign owners and deadlines.**
Every corrective action needs a specific person responsible and a specific completion date. "We will look into this" is not a corrective action.

**Step 7: Follow up.**
Verify corrective actions were actually implemented. Close the loop in writing.

The purpose of investigation is prevention, not blame. If your investigation ends with "the employee made an error," you have not investigated — you have scapegoated. Systems fail before people do. Find the system failure.
