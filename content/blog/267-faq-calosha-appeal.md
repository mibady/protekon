title: "FAQ: Can I appeal a Cal/OSHA citation?"
faqCategory: cal-osha
excerpt: "Yes, you can appeal a Cal/OSHA citation within 15 working days through an informal conference or formal OSHAB appeal. Missing the deadline makes the citation final and unappealable."
---

Yes, you can appeal. But you have exactly 15 working days from receipt of the citation. Miss that window and the citation becomes final, unappealable, and fully enforceable. No exceptions.

**Your two appeal options:**

**Option 1: Informal conference.**

Request an informal conference with the Cal/OSHA district manager. This is a meeting -- not a legal proceeding -- where you can present evidence, challenge the citation classification, negotiate penalty reductions, or discuss abatement timelines. Many citations are resolved here without formal proceedings. It is faster, cheaper, and less adversarial than a formal appeal.

The informal conference does not extend your 15-day appeal deadline. If the conference does not resolve the issue, you must still file a formal appeal within the original timeframe.

**Option 2: Formal appeal to OSHAB.**

File a formal appeal with the Occupational Safety and Health Appeals Board. This initiates a legal proceeding with hearings, evidence, witnesses, and a formal decision. You will likely need legal representation. The process can take months to over a year.

**What you can challenge:**

- Whether the violation actually occurred
- The classification (e.g., serious vs. general)
- The penalty amount
- The abatement deadline

**What most employers get wrong:**

They ignore the citation, assume it will go away, or decide to "deal with it later." There is no later. Fifteen working days. That is three calendar weeks. After that, the citation is final order. You pay the penalty. You abate the hazard. You have no legal recourse.

When you receive a citation, the clock starts immediately. Read it that day. Decide your response that week. Act within two weeks. The 15-day deadline is absolute.
