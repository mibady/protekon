# Understanding Your Compliance Dashboard

Your Protekon dashboard is your compliance command center. Every widget, score, and alert on this page exists to answer one question: are you ready for an inspection right now?

Here is what every section means, how to read it, and what to do when something turns red.

## Compliance Score Widget

The large circular score at the top of your dashboard is your overall compliance score. It ranges from 0 to 100 and is calculated from four weighted components:

| Component | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Active Plans | 30% | Do you have current, reviewed WVPP, IIPP, and vertical-specific plans? |
| Assessment Status | 25% | Have you completed and kept current your Phase 1 and Phase 2 assessments? |
| Training Completion | 25% | Are all employees current on required training? |
| Incident Response | 20% | Are incidents being reported, investigated, and closed in a timely manner? |

**Score interpretation:**

- **90-100 (Green):** Strong compliance posture. Maintain current practices and review quarterly.
- **70-89 (Yellow):** Gaps exist. Review the component breakdown to identify which area is dragging your score down.
- **Below 70 (Red):** Significant exposure. Address critical gaps immediately. You are not ready for an inspection.

Click the score widget to see the component breakdown and identify exactly which items need attention.

## Enforcement Alerts

The Enforcement Alerts panel shows active regulatory developments relevant to your industry and jurisdiction. Protekon monitors federal, state, and local regulatory activity and filters it to show only what affects you.

Each alert includes:

- **Severity** (High, Medium, Low) — based on the potential penalty exposure and compliance timeline
- **Jurisdiction** — Federal, your state, or local
- **Standard** — The specific regulation affected
- **Action Required** — What you need to do and by when

Alerts with a red badge require immediate action. Yellow badges indicate upcoming deadlines. Click any alert to read the full regulatory update with compliance guidance.

## Upcoming Deadlines

The Deadlines panel aggregates all time-sensitive compliance obligations into a single timeline view:

- **Training renewals** — Employees whose annual training is due within 30, 60, or 90 days
- **Plan review dates** — Annual review deadlines for your WVPP, IIPP, and vertical programs
- **Regulatory filing deadlines** — OSHA 300A posting (February 1 through April 30), electronic submission (March 2), and state-specific filings
- **Assessment refresh dates** — Quarterly reassessment reminders

Items within 30 days display in red. Items within 31 to 60 days display in yellow. Items beyond 60 days display in green. You can export your deadline calendar as an ICS file to import into Google Calendar, Outlook, or Apple Calendar.

## Training Status

The Training Status panel provides a real-time view of your organization's training compliance:

- **Overall completion rate** — Percentage of employees with all required training current
- **Overdue training** — Number of employees with expired training, broken down by training type
- **Upcoming training** — Scheduled sessions in the next 30 days
- **Training by type** — Completion rates for each required training category (WVPP, IIPP, heat illness, vertical-specific, etc.)

Click any training category to see the specific employees who are overdue or upcoming. You can send reminder notifications directly from this panel.

## Document Library Quick Access

The Document Library section provides one-click access to your most important compliance documents:

- **WVPP** — Your current Workplace Violence Prevention Plan with last-reviewed date
- **IIPP** — Your current Injury and Illness Prevention Program with last-reviewed date
- **Vertical Programs** — Industry-specific plans (HIPAA SRA, heat illness plan, silica exposure control, etc.)
- **Training Records** — Aggregated training documentation by session
- **Incident Reports** — Filed incident reports and investigation records

Each document shows its version number, last-modified date, and review status. Documents overdue for annual review are flagged with a warning icon.

## Incident Log Summary

The Incident Log panel shows:

- **Total incidents** this month, quarter, and year
- **Open investigations** — Incidents reported but not yet fully investigated and closed
- **Average time to close** — How long it takes from report to completed investigation
- **Incident types** — Breakdown by category (injury, near-miss, threat, property damage)

A healthy incident log is not an empty one. It is one that shows incidents being reported, investigated promptly, and closed with corrective actions. Zero reported incidents in a 100-person company does not mean zero incidents — it means your employees are not reporting.

## Industry Benchmarks

The Benchmarks panel compares your compliance metrics against anonymized, aggregated data from other Protekon customers in your industry:

- **Your compliance score vs. industry average**
- **Your training completion rate vs. industry average**
- **Your incident response time vs. industry average**

This is not a competition. It is context. If your score is 75 and the industry average is 88, you know you have work to do. If your score is 92 and the industry average is 71, you know you are ahead — but you should not stop.

## Dashboard Actions

From your dashboard, you can take immediate action:

- **"Run Assessment"** — Start or continue a compliance assessment
- **"Schedule Training"** — Create a new training session
- **"Report Incident"** — File a new incident report
- **"Generate Report"** — Export your compliance status for stakeholders, insurers, or inspectors

Your dashboard is live. Every action you take in Protekon immediately updates your scores, deadlines, and alerts. Use it daily. Check it before every meeting with your insurance broker. Pull it up when your landlord asks about your safety program. And definitely have it ready when an inspector walks through your door.
