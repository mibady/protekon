# FAQ: What are the different citation severity levels?

Cal/OSHA citations are not all created equal. The severity level determines the penalty, and the penalties escalate fast. Know the tiers.

**Regulatory (lowest severity):**
Technical violations — missing postings, incomplete paperwork, minor recordkeeping errors. Low penalties, but they signal that your program has gaps. Inspectors take note.

**General — up to $18,000 per violation:**
A hazard exists that could cause injury or illness, but it is not likely to cause death or serious physical harm. These are the "you should have known better" citations. Unguarded equipment, inadequate housekeeping, missing labels.

**Serious — up to $25,000 per violation:**
A hazard exists that the employer knew about or should have known about, and it could cause death or serious physical harm. This is the most common citation issued during inspections. "Should have known" is a low bar — if the hazard was visible and you did not address it, you should have known.

**Willful — up to $180,000 per violation:**
The employer intentionally and knowingly violated a standard, or showed plain indifference to employee safety. This is where the math gets ugly. One willful violation costs more than many businesses spend on their entire safety program for a decade.

**Repeat:**
Same or substantially similar violation within 5 years of a previous citation becoming final. Penalties are enhanced significantly. Cal/OSHA treats repeat violations as evidence that you did not take the first citation seriously.

**The real lesson:** The jump from General to Serious is about knowledge. The jump from Serious to Willful is about intent. And Repeat citations tell the inspector you learned nothing.

Every citation is a permanent record. It follows your business. Insurance carriers see it. Future inspectors reference it. Potential clients check it.

Fix hazards before they become citations. It is always cheaper.
