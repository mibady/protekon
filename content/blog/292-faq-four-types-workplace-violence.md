# FAQ: What are the four types of workplace violence?

California's WVPP framework recognizes four distinct types of workplace violence. Your plan must address every type that is relevant to your operation. Most employers only think about one or two. That is a compliance gap.

**Type 1: Criminal Intent**
The perpetrator has no legitimate relationship to the business. Think robbery, trespassing, active shooter with no employee connection. Retail, convenience stores, gas stations, and businesses that handle cash are highest risk.

Prevention focuses on environmental design: lighting, visibility, cash handling procedures, security systems, and access control.

**Type 2: Customer/Client**
Violence from someone receiving services — a patient, customer, client, student, or inmate. Healthcare workers, social services, education, and public-facing roles face this daily.

Prevention focuses on de-escalation training, staffing levels, patient/client screening, and physical barriers where appropriate.

**Type 3: Worker-on-Worker**
Violence between current or former employees. Bullying, harassment, intimidation, threats, and physical assault from coworkers or supervisors. This is the type most employers want to pretend does not exist at their company.

Prevention focuses on anti-harassment policies, reporting mechanisms, management training, and a culture where threats are taken seriously the first time.

**Type 4: Personal Relationship**
Domestic violence that spills into the workplace. An abusive partner shows up at the job site. This is the type employers feel least equipped to handle — but it is their legal responsibility under the WVPP.

Prevention focuses on confidential reporting, safety planning with affected employees, restraining order enforcement, and security awareness.

**Your obligation:** Assess which types pose the greatest risk for your specific workplace and tailor your prevention strategies accordingly. A construction site faces different risks than a medical clinic. Your WVPP must reflect your reality, not a generic template.

All four types. Assess all four. Address all relevant ones.
