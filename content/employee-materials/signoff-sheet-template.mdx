## Section 1 — Acknowledgment of Receipt

I, **{{employee.name}}**, acknowledge that I have received a copy of the Workplace Violence Prevention Plan (WVPP) for {{client.business_name}}. I understand that:

- The plan is required by California Senate Bill 553 (Cal. Lab. Code §6401.9).
- I may review the full plan at any time by asking my supervisor or the safety coordinator.
- The plan applies to me whenever I am performing work for {{client.business_name}}.
- I may not retaliate against any coworker who reports a workplace violence incident or concern.
- I will not be disciplined, demoted, or terminated for making a good-faith report.

## Section 2 — Confirmation of Training

I confirm that I have completed the workplace violence prevention training required by the plan. The training covered:

- The four types of workplace violence (Type 1–4) and how to recognize each.
- How to report incidents, threats, and suspicious activity through the channels described in the plan.
- Emergency response procedures, including evacuation, lockdown, and calling for help.
- De-escalation techniques appropriate to my role.
- My right to participate in plan reviews and to be free from retaliation.
- The location and retention of the Violent Incident Log and how privacy is protected.

I had the opportunity to ask questions. My questions were answered to my satisfaction.

## Section 3 — Ongoing Obligations

I understand that I am expected to:

- Report every incident, threat, and near-miss — even if no one was harmed.
- Follow the emergency procedures in the plan.
- Attend refresher training at least once a year and whenever the plan changes.
- Notify my supervisor if I see a new hazard or believe the plan is not working.
