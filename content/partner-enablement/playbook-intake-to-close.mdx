# Playbook — Intake to Close

This is the motion. Five steps. Every partner who hits a healthy book runs this same sequence. Deviating is fine once you know why you are deviating; do not freelance until you have run it clean ten times.

## Step 1 — Prospect

You are not prospecting cold. You are prospecting your existing book first, then warm referrals, then — only if you must — cold industries you have never served.

Your best prospect looks like this:

- Has employees (even one).
- Holds a lease, a license, or a regulated permit.
- Is in a jurisdiction you already serve.
- Has been on your roster for at least six months.
- Has mentioned, in passing, anything about OSHA, HIPAA, SB 553, workplace violence, audits, insurance renewals, EDD letters, or posters.

Open the conversation with a specific, current regulatory event. Not "compliance is complicated." That is a feature brochure. You say:

> "Cal/OSHA opened a workplace violence prevention audit cycle last quarter. Every employer with 10+ employees in California needs an SB 553 plan on file. I'm offering our clients a free compliance score — ten questions, five minutes, and you'll see exactly where you stand. Want me to send the link?"

If they say yes, you send the intake link. That is the entire first touch.

## Step 2 — Intake Assessment

The intake is the product. Ten questions, branded to you, scored instantly. Three outcomes determine your next move:

- **Score 80–100.** Low gap. Say so, send the report, check back in 90 days. Do not force a sale. These clients become case studies and referrals.
- **Score 50–79.** Moderate gap. This is your sweet spot. Schedule a 20-minute score review call.
- **Score 0–49.** High gap. Call them the same day. This prospect has exposure and knows it.

Never send the score report without a follow-up booked. The report is the permission slip to have the proposal conversation.

## Step 3 — Discovery Call (20 minutes)

You are not pitching. You are confirming the gap, sizing the pain, and pricing to the pain.

Run these six questions, in this order, before you say a single thing about price:

1. **"Walk me through what happens today when OSHA/HIPAA/the DOL sends you a letter."** Listen for: panic, delegation to an assistant, calling their attorney, "it hasn't happened yet." The more nervous, the higher the tier.
2. **"Who on your team owns compliance documentation today?"** Listen for: "nobody," "the office manager does her best," "I do it at night." That is your wedge. Every one of those answers means you are the fix.
3. **"How many sites, and how many employees at each?"** This sizes the tier and the multi-site overage. Write the numbers down.
4. **"What verticals do you operate in?"** Construction, healthcare, manufacturing, etc. Each adds a module. Each justifies Professional over Essentials.
5. **"When was the last time your employee handbook, SB 553 plan, or HIPAA risk assessment was updated?"** If the answer is "I'm not sure" or "more than a year ago," the remediation scope is wide. Setup fee goes up accordingly.
6. **"If I could hand you a compliance officer who keeps all of this current in the background, and you only heard from them when something needed your sign-off — what would that be worth to you?"** Wait. Let them answer first. Their answer is your pricing ceiling.

Close the call with: "I'll put together a scoped proposal today. You'll have it in your inbox before end of day tomorrow. If it fits, we start next Monday."

## Step 4 — Proposal

The proposal is one page. Maximum two. It reads like an operator wrote it, because one did.

Use this template verbatim. Fill in the brackets.

> **Proposal — [Client Name] Ongoing Compliance**
>
> Prepared by [Partner Firm] · [Date]
>
> **What you get**
>
> [Client] receives an AI Compliance Officer, supported by [Partner Firm], covering:
> - [List the 3–5 specific gaps from the intake score]
> - Ongoing regulatory monitoring for [jurisdiction + vertical]
> - Incident log and audit-ready documentation trail
> - Monthly compliance review with [Partner Name]
> - [Any multi-site or specialty add-ons]
>
> **Investment**
>
> - One-time setup: **$[X]**. Covers initial document library build, site onboarding, and historical gap remediation.
> - Monthly retainer: **$[Y]**. Covers the AI Officer engagement, regulatory monitoring, incident handling, and monthly review.
> - [If multi-site] Additional sites: $197/site/month beyond the first.
>
> **Timeline**
>
> - Week 1: Setup. You sign, we onboard, document library is live.
> - Week 2: First regulatory digest delivered. First incident log entry.
> - Week 4: First monthly review.
>
> **Term**
>
> Month-to-month. Thirty-day notice to cancel. No long-term contract.
>
> **Next step**
>
> Reply "Go" to this email and I will send the Stripe link for the setup fee. We start the following Monday.

That is the whole proposal. No PDF. No cover letter. No twelve-slide deck. Operators respect brevity.

## Step 5 — Close and Onboard

When they reply "Go":

- Send the Stripe link for setup **within one hour**. Momentum is the close.
- Once setup is paid, provision them in the partner portal. This takes you under ten minutes.
- Schedule the Week 1 kickoff for a 30-minute Zoom. Walk them through the portal. Introduce the AI Officer. Confirm who on their side is the point of contact.
- Schedule the first monthly review on their calendar before you end the kickoff. Recurring, same day each month.

That is the close. No negotiation theater. No discounting. No stretched-out sales cycle.

## Pricing anchoring

When the prospect says "that seems like a lot," you anchor. Memorize three anchors:

- **Anchor 1 — the lawyer.** "An employment attorney bills $450 an hour. One SB 553 plan is four hours of billable time. That is $1,800 for one document that I refresh every quarter for you, included."
- **Anchor 2 — the fine.** "The average Cal/OSHA citation for a missing workplace violence plan is $18,000. Your annual investment here is under $11,000. One avoided citation pays for three years."
- **Anchor 3 — the breach.** "A single HIPAA breach averages $137 per record. A 200-patient practice with a lost laptop is a $27,000 event. This engagement costs less than one such event, every year, forever."

You are not the cheapest option. You are not trying to be. You are the only option that is recurring, branded, and operated by a human they already trust.

## What a good week looks like

A partner in flow runs this rhythm:

- Monday: send two intake links from the existing book.
- Tuesday–Wednesday: follow up on any intake submitted the prior week.
- Thursday: one discovery call, one proposal drafted.
- Friday: send the proposal, request the Stripe link, schedule next week's close.

One close per week is twelve new retainer clients per quarter. That is a practice.
