# The Protekon Practice

Compliance is a recurring, high-anxiety, low-expertise problem for every business with employees, a lease, or a license. It is also a problem most operators do not want to solve themselves. That gap is your practice.

Protekon gives you an **AI Compliance Officer** you can resell to your existing clients. Not a compliance consultancy. Not a generic SaaS subscription. An always-on officer, branded to you, that drafts the policies, flags the regulatory changes, tracks the incidents, and produces the documentation auditors actually ask for.

This is the same service model that works for fractional CFOs, fractional CMOs, and MSPs. The client does not want another seat license. They want a named person (and now, a named AI) who owns the outcome. You are that named person. The platform does the fulfillment.

## Who this is for

Four operator profiles consistently win with this practice:

- **MSPs and IT consultancies.** You already hold the compliance conversation around HIPAA, SOC 2, and cyber insurance attestations. Layering an AI Compliance Officer onto that conversation is a five-minute close. Most MSPs see a 20–35% attach rate on existing managed-services clients within the first quarter.
- **Accountants, bookkeepers, and fractional CFOs.** Your clients already trust you with the numbers. When the EDD, Cal/OSHA, or DOL letter arrives, they call you first. Today you refer them out. With Protekon you capture that revenue and keep the relationship closed-loop.
- **HR consultancies and PEO resellers.** Employee handbooks, I-9 files, harassment training, SB 553 plans — every one of those is a document Protekon generates and keeps current. You stop authoring Word docs at 9pm and start shipping branded deliverables in under a minute.
- **Boutique safety and environmental shops.** You have the domain credibility. You lack the software. Protekon is the back office: intake scoring, regulatory feed, incident log, audit packets, client portal. You keep the advisory work. We keep the paperwork alive.

If you sell anything recurring to a small or mid-market business, you can sell this. The clients who need it are already on your roster.

## What you actually sell

You sell three things, in this order:

1. **A ten-question intake assessment.** Free. The prospect answers in five minutes. They get a compliance score, a gap list, and a proposed remediation plan. This is your door-opener and your best single sales asset.
2. **An ongoing AI Compliance Officer engagement.** Monthly retainer. The AI Officer maintains the documents, monitors the regulatory feed for their jurisdiction and industry, logs incidents, and produces audit-ready packages on demand. You are the named human accountable to the client.
3. **Specialty add-ons.** Multi-site ($197 per additional site per month), vertical deep-dives (construction subcontractor management, HIPAA BAA tracking, poster compliance rollups), and on-demand audit prep packages.

The product is the same for every client. The wrapper — your logo, your domain, your voice — is what makes it yours.

## Pricing tiers

Protekon runs three client-facing tiers plus a multi-site overage. These are the numbers you quote:

- **Essentials — $597/month.** One site. Up to 10 employees. Core document library, regulatory alerts, incident log, quarterly audit packet.
- **Professional — $897/month.** One site. Up to 50 employees. Everything in Essentials plus vertical modules (construction, healthcare, manufacturing, retail, transportation, wholesale, hospitality, agriculture, real estate), training records, and monthly compliance review.
- **Enterprise — $1,297/month.** One site. 50+ employees. Professional plus priority support, custom policy authoring, enforcement monitoring, and dedicated onboarding.
- **Multi-Site overage — +$197 per additional site per month.** Applies across any tier. A 4-site Professional client is $897 + (3 × $197) = $1,488/month.

Setup fees scale with the work. A single-site Essentials client is a flat $497 setup. A multi-site Enterprise client with custom verticals can be $2,500–$5,000 setup. You collect setup on day one. Retainer starts day 31.

**You never commodity-price this.** If a prospect pushes for $199/month, the answer is no. That client belongs to a compliance-poster vendor, not you. Your buyer values the ongoing relationship and the AI Officer doing the work in the background, and they will pay to keep it that way.

## Deal economics

The numbers a partner quotes internally:

- **Platform fee (your cost): 40–50% of retail** depending on partner tier. Your spread is the rest.
- **Gross margin per client: $300–$750/month** after platform fee, before your time.
- **Fulfillment time per client per month: 30–60 minutes.** Review the regulatory digest, approve the auto-drafted documents, handle one or two client questions. The AI Officer does the writing. You do the judgment calls.
- **Break-even: 4–6 clients.** This covers the platform fee, your sales time, and leaves margin.
- **Healthy book: 15–25 clients.** At a 20-client Professional book, a partner nets $6,000–$9,000/month in recurring margin on under 20 hours per month of fulfillment work.
- **Churn: under 8% annual** among partners who run monthly reviews. Compliance clients are sticky because the switching cost is the documentation trail — and that trail lives in your portal.

A partner who closes two clients a month hits a healthy book inside a year. A partner who bundles this into existing MSP or accounting engagements hits it in one quarter.

## What a practice is not

A practice is not a referral arrangement. If you hand the client off, you do not have a practice — you have a finder fee. Protekon partners own the relationship, own the invoice, own the brand on the deliverables, and own the renewal conversation. We are the back office. You are the firm.

A practice is not a product resale. You are not selling software licenses by the seat. You are selling the outcome — "your compliance is handled" — and pricing it to the value of that outcome, not to the cost of the underlying tooling.

A practice is not a side hustle. It is a line of revenue that compounds quietly. Ten clients in year one. Twenty-five by year three. At that point it is a business by itself, running alongside whatever else you do, and it is the single cleanest recurring revenue stream most operators will ever build.
