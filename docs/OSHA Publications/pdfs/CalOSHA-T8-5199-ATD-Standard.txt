## (a) Scope and Application.

(1) Scope. This section applies to work in the following facilities, service categories, or operations:

(A) Each of the following health care facilities, services, or operations:
1. Hospitals
2. Skilled nursing facilities
3. Clinics, medical offices, and other outpatient medical facilities
4. Facilities where high hazard procedures, as defined in subsection (b), are performed
5. Home health care
6. Long term health care facilities and hospices
7. Medical outreach services
8. Paramedic and emergency medical services including these services when provided by firefighters and other emergency responders
9. Medical transport

(B) Facilities, services, or operations that are designated to receive persons arriving from the scene of an uncontrolled release of hazardous substances involving biological agents.

(C) Police services, provided during transport or detention of persons reasonably anticipated to be cases or suspected cases of aerosol transmissible diseases.

(D) Public health services, such as communicable disease contact tracing or screening programs.

(E) The following facilities, services or operations that are identified as being at increased risk for transmission of ATD infection:
1. Correctional facilities and other facilities that house inmates or detainees
2. Homeless shelters
3. Drug treatment programs

(F) Facilities, services or operations that perform aerosol-generating procedures on cadavers.

(G) Laboratories that perform procedures with materials that contain or are reasonably anticipated to contain ATP-L or zoonotic ATPs.

(H) Any other facility determined in writing by the Chief of DOSH through an Order to Take Special Action.

(I) Maintenance, renovation, service, or repair operations involving air handling systems or equipment or building areas that may reasonably be anticipated to be contaminated with ATPs.

(2) The following are not covered by this standard:
(A) Outpatient dental clinics or offices (if conditions met)
(B) Outpatient medical specialty practices (if conditions met)

(3) Application.
(A) Referring Employers — reduced requirements (screening, referral, source control)
(B) Laboratories — comply with subsections (a), (f), (i) and (j)
(C) All other covered employers — comply with subsections (a), (d), (e), (f), (g), (h), (i), and (j)

## (b) Key Definitions

- Aerosol transmissible disease (ATD): A disease for which droplet or airborne precautions are required, as listed in Appendix A.
- Aerosol transmissible pathogen (ATP): A pathogen for which droplet or airborne precautions are required.
- ATP-L (Laboratory): A pathogen listed in Appendix D or requiring BSL-3+.
- Airborne infection isolation (AII): Procedures to reduce risk of airborne pathogen transmission.
- AIIR: Room maintained at negative pressure to contain airborne pathogens.
- AirID: Aerosol transmissible disease transmitted via airborne droplet nuclei.
- Biological safety officer: Qualified person authorized to implement biosafety controls.
- Case: Person diagnosed by health care provider or meeting CDC case definitions.
- Exposure incident: Event with exposure to RATD case without required controls.
- High hazard procedures: Sputum induction, bronchoscopy, aerosolized medications, PFT, autopsy, etc.
- Novel or unknown ATP: Newly recognized pathogen capable of serious disease via aerosol.
- Occupational exposure: Work exposure creating elevated risk of ATD infection.
- Referring employer: Employer that screens and refers AirID cases, does not treat/house them.
- Suspected case: Person believed to probably have a disease listed in Appendix A.

## (c) Referring Employers

Referring employers must:
1. Designate infection control administrator
2. Implement source control procedures (Respiratory Hygiene/Cough Etiquette)
3. Establish screening and referral procedures (transfer within 5 hours)
4. Communicate with employees and other employers regarding disease status
5. Implement risk reduction measures during referral period
6. Provide medical services (vaccinations, TB surveillance, exposure incident procedures, seasonal flu)
7. Train all employees with occupational exposure
8. Annual review of infection control procedures
9. Maintain records

---

## (d) ATD Exposure Control Plan

Required elements:
(A) Named administrator(s) knowledgeable in infection control
(B) Job classifications with occupational exposure
(C) High hazard procedures list
(D) Tasks requiring PPE/respiratory protection
(E) Implementation methods for engineering controls, PPE, decontamination
(F) Source control measures
(G) Procedures for identifying, isolating, and referring/transferring AirID cases
(H) Medical services procedures including vaccinations
(I) Exposure incident procedures
(J) Exposure incident evaluation and corrective action
(K) Communication procedures with employees and other employers
(L) Inter-employer communication for exposure incidents
(M) Adequate PPE supply procedures
(N) Training procedures
(O) Recordkeeping procedures
(P) Employee involvement in plan review
(Q) Surge procedures for epidemics/emergencies

Plan must be reviewed annually with employee involvement.

## (e) Engineering and Work Practice Controls, and PPE

Key requirements:
- Use feasible engineering and work practice controls to minimize ATP exposure
- Implement source control procedures (Respiratory Hygiene/Cough Etiquette)
- Develop vehicle transport controls with barriers and air handling
- Written decontamination procedures
- Inform contractors about infectious disease hazards
- AirID cases must be identified, masked, and placed in AII within 5 hours

### AIIR Requirements:
- Negative pressure maintained
- 12+ ACH (minimum 6 ACH outdoor air)
- Daily smoke trail verification while in use
- Annual inspection and performance monitoring
- Air exhausted directly outside or HEPA filtered before recirculation
- Ducts under negative pressure before HEPA filtration
- Doors/windows kept closed
- Room ventilated per CDC Table 1 (99.9% removal) before unprotected entry

---

## (f) Laboratories

- Biological safety officer performs risk assessment per BMBL methodology
- Written Biosafety Plan (BSP) required
- BSP elements: safety officer, job classifications, ATP-L list, containment equipment, safe handling, decontamination, PPE, respiratory protection, emergency procedures, medical services, training, annual inspections

---

## (g) Respiratory Protection

- NIOSH-approved respirators required
- Written respiratory protection program per Section 5144
- Minimum N95 for airborne precautions
- PAPR with HEPA for high hazard procedures (effective 9/1/2010)
- Medical evaluation before fit testing
- Annual fit testing required
- Annual training per Section 5144

Required use situations:
- Entering AIIR in use
- Present during AirID case procedures
- Repairing air systems that may contain ATPs
- Working in area after AirID case leaves (per decontamination requirements)
- Working in residence with known AirID case
- Aerosol-generating procedures on infected cadavers
- Tasks specified in Biosafety Plan
- Transporting unmasked AirID case in enclosed vehicle

---

## (h) Medical Services

Key requirements:
- TB surveillance: Annual TB testing for all employees with occupational exposure
- TB conversions: Referral to knowledgeable PLHCP, diagnostic testing, treatment options
- Vaccinations: All vaccines in Appendix E for susceptible healthcare workers (within 10 days of assignment)
- Seasonal influenza vaccine for all employees with occupational exposure
- Exposure incident procedures:
  - Report RATD cases to local health officer
  - Notify other employers within 72 hours
  - Conduct exposure analysis within 72 hours
  - Notify exposed employees within 96 hours
  - Provide post-exposure medical evaluation
- Precautionary removal with full pay/benefits if PLHCP recommends
- Written PLHCP opinion within 15 working days

---

## (i) Training

Required for all employees with occupational exposure:
- At initial assignment and annually thereafter
- Content must be appropriate to education level, literacy, and language

Minimum training elements:
(A) General explanation of ATDs and signs/symptoms
(B) Modes of transmission and source control
(C) ATD Exposure Control Plan / Biosafety Plan
(D) Recognizing exposure tasks
(E) Engineering controls, work practices, decontamination, PPE
(F) PPE selection, use, limitations
(G) TB surveillance procedures
(H) Respirator training per Section 5144
(I) Vaccine information
(J) Exposure incident procedures
(K) Surge plan information
(L) Interactive Q&A opportunity (within 24 hours if not in-person)

---

## (j) Recordkeeping

### Medical Records:
- Employee name/identifier
- Vaccination status and declination forms
- Written PLHCP opinions and TB assessments
- Exposure incident information
- Kept confidential, maintained employment + 30 years

### Training Records:
- Dates, contents/summary, trainer names/qualifications, attendee names/titles
- Maintained 3 years

### Implementation Records:
- Annual plan review records (3 years)
- Exposure incident records (per Section 3204)
- Vaccine unavailability records (3 years)
- AII room unavailability records (3 years)
- Patient transfer decision records (3 years)
- Engineering control inspection/testing/maintenance (5 years)
- Respiratory protection program records (per Section 5144)
