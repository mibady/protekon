EXECUTIVE SUMMARYPROTEKON (protekon.vercel.app) is a managed Compliance-as-a-Service (CaaS) platform targeting California small and mid-sized businesses (10–500 employees). The platform currently sells directly to end businesses, providing recurring, done-for-you physical workplace compliance across multiple regulatory domains.The strategic evolution is to transform PROTEKON from a direct-to-business service into a white-label compliance platform that channel partners (insurance brokers, PEOs, safety consultants, HR firms, industry associations, business attorneys) can use to deliver compliance services under their own brand to their existing client base.This is modeled after ScalePad's ControlMap product, which provides a white-label GRC platform to IT Managed Service Providers (MSPs) who then resell cybersecurity compliance services to their SMB clients. PROTEKON adapts this model for physical workplace compliance (not cybersecurity) across all target verticals.The end state: A partner logs in, sees all their clients in an aggregate dashboard, onboards new clients into any vertical, manages compliance delivery, and the end client sees the partner's brand throughout the experience. PROTEKON powers the engine; the partner owns the relationship.---## 2. THE WEDGE: WHY PROTEKON WINSEvery other option on the market makes the business owner do the compliance work. PROTEKON does the compliance work. That's it. That's the entire wedge.### What Every Alternative Actually Sells**Training companies** sell you a course to watch. When it's over, you still have to write the plan, set up the incident log, strip the PII, store the records, and figure out what changed when a new regulation drops. You paid $20/seat and you're right back where you started — except now you've spent money and still have nothing to show an inspector.**Template providers** sell you a PDF with blank fields. CalChamber's SB 553 kit is $1,298. You get a checklist and some fill-in-the-blank documents. Nobody tells you what to write in those blanks. Nobody processes your incidents when they happen. Nobody updates the plan when the law changes. You bought a homework assignment.**Enterprise GRC software** — NAVEX, Resolver, FaceUp — gives you a dashboard. A very expensive dashboard, $800-$2,000+ a month, that you and your IT team get to log into and manage yourselves. You're still the one entering data, interpreting regulations, generating reports, and maintaining records. You just bought a more sophisticated place to do the same work you were already failing to do. And you need staff who know how to operate it.**Local compliance consultants** charge $150-$300/hour. They'll write your plan, bill you for it, and disappear. When you have an incident at 11pm on a Saturday, they're not there. When a regulation changes six months later, you're paying another invoice to update documents. No ongoing coverage, no system, no records management — just hourly billing for episodic work.### What PROTEKON Actually DoesPROTEKON is the only option where the client's job is to answer 6 questions and then go run their business.**Document generation:** The system writes the plan — not from a template, from AI that generates a site-specific document using the client's actual address, actual worksite layout, actual industry, actual employee count.**Incident processing:** When an incident happens, the client describes what happened in plain English. The AI classifies the severity, assigns the OSHA code, flags PII, strips it, stores the record for 5 years, notifies the compliance officer, and schedules follow-ups based on severity. The client never touches a log.**Regulatory monitoring:** When Cal/OSHA publishes a new regulation, the system scans the RSS feed at 6am, runs it through the AI regulatory analyzer, determines the impact level, identifies which client documents are affected, and sends an alert with a plain-English summary and a compliance deadline. The client doesn't have to monitor anything.**Automated delivery:** Every week, month, quarter, and year — branded compliance reports land in the client's inbox. Not reminders to go do something. Proof that it's already done.### Who This Is ForThe 30-person warehouse, the three-location auto shop, the family-owned construction company — these businesses don't have a compliance officer. They don't have an IT team. They don't have time to learn a platform. They need someone to handle it. That's what the $597/mo buys. Not access to a system. Not a course to take. Not a template to fill out. The actual compliance, managed and delivered, with an audit trail that proves it.### Competitive Positioning Summary| Alternative | What They Sell | What the Client Still Has to Do | Price ||------------|---------------|-------------------------------|-------|| Training company | Course to watch | Write plan, set up logs, strip PII, store records, monitor regs | $20/seat || Template provider | PDF with blanks | Fill in the blanks, process incidents, update when law changes | $1,298 one-time || Enterprise GRC | Dashboard | Enter data, interpret regs, generate reports, maintain records, staff IT | $800-$2,000+/mo || Local consultant | Hourly advice | Wait for availability, pay per update, no system, no records mgmt | $150-$300/hr || **PROTEKON** | **Done-for-you compliance** | **Answer 6 questions. The wedge — done-for-you, not done-by-you — is what the partner is actually reselling.---## 3. INSPIRATION MODEL: ScalePad / ControlMap### What ControlMap DoesControlMap is a compliance management platform built exclusively for IT MSPs. Key facts:- Acquired by ScalePad in March 2023 (team: 150+ employees)- Supports 60+ compliance frameworks (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA IT controls, CMMC, etc.)- 40+ native integrations (AWS, Azure, Okta, BambooHR, etc.)- 50+ audit-ready policy/control templates- Automated evidence collection from integrated systems- Executive Dashboard with compliance health scores and Trust Portal- Pricing: $200/mo (MSP self-compliance) | $400+/mo (CaaS per-client package)- Tiered onboarding: Free (1 assessment) → Essentials (1 framework) → Full CaaS- "Compliance Boot Camp" — hands-on training teaching MSPs to package, price, and pitch CaaS### ControlMap's Business Model```ScalePad/ControlMap (platform vendor)        │        ├── Sells platform license to MSP ($200-400+/mo per client)        │        └── MSP (channel partner)                │                ├── White-labels the platform                ├── Adds their margin ($200-600+ markup)                ├── Manages client onboarding and delivery                │                └── SMB End Client                        │                        └── Sees MSP's brand, pays MSP directly```### What Makes ControlMap Work1. **Channel leverage** — 500 MSPs × 20 clients each = 10,000 end clients acquired through channel2. **Framework cross-mapping** — one evidence artifact satisfies multiple compliance frameworks, reducing duplication3. **Existing channel identity** — ~40,000 MSPs in the US already self-identify as IT service providers; ControlMap gives them a compliance add-on revenue stream4. **Enablement program** — Boot Camp teaches MSPs how to build a practice, not just use a tool5. **Tiered adoption** — Free tier lets MSPs prove value before committing### Key Differences: ControlMap vs. PROTEKON| Dimension | ControlMap (IT Compliance) | PROTEKON (Physical Workplace Compliance) ||-----------|--------------------------|----------------------------------------|| Compliance type | Digital/cybersecurity (SOC 2, ISO, CMMC) | Physical workplace (OSHA, SB 553, Cal/OSHA, HIPAA, wage & hour) || Evidence collection | API-driven (pulls from AWS, Azure, Okta) | Document-driven (written plans, incident logs, PII scrubbing, PDF output) || Automation surface | High — data lives in systems with APIs | Medium — requires AI document generation, municipal scraping, regulatory monitoring || Geographic scope | Frameworks are global/national standards | Jurisdictionally fragmented (California state, city-by-city ordinances) || Channel partner | MSPs (established category, ~40K in US) | Fragmented: insurance brokers, PEOs, safety consultants, HR firms, trade associations, attorneys || Moat | Network effects, integrations | Jurisdictional complexity, regulatory depth, OSHA enforcement data || Pricing to partner | $200-400+/mo per client | Target: $149-399/mo per client (partner prices end client at $300-800/mo) |---## 4.
